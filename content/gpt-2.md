GPT-2 is the 2nd [GPT](/wiki/gpt) [model](/wiki/model) released by [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) in February 2019 with the [paper](/wiki/paper) [Language Models are Unsupervised Multitask Learners](/index.php?title=Language_Models_are_Unsupervised_Multitask_Learners&action=edit&redlink=1). Although it is larger than its predecessor, [GPT-1](/wiki/gpt-1), it is very similar. The main difference is that GPT-2 can [multitask](/index.php?title=Multitask&action=edit&redlink=1). It is able to perform well on multiple tasks without being [trained](/index.php?title=Trained&action=edit&redlink=1) on any [examples](/wiki/examples). GPT-2 demonstrated that [language model](/wiki/language_model) could better comprehend [natural language](/index.php?title=Natural_language&action=edit&redlink=1) and perform better on more tasks when it is trained on a larger [dataset](/wiki/dataset) and when it has more [parameters](/index.php?title=Parameters&action=edit&redlink=1); the larger the language model is, the better it is. With over 1.5 billion parameters (10 times greater than GPT-1), GPT-2's performance exceeded the state-of-the-art for many tasks in [zero shot](/index.php?title=Zero_shot&action=edit&redlink=1) settings. To get the [training data](/wiki/training_data), the authors used [Reddit](/index.php?title=Reddit&action=edit&redlink=1) to scrape article text from the outbound links of Reddit posts that had received upvotes. This created a large, high-quality, and extensive [dataset](/wiki/dataset) called the [WebText](/index.php?title=WebText&action=edit&redlink=1). The dataset contained 40GB of text from over 8 million documents, which is far greater than the amount of data GPT-1 was trained on. GPT-2 was tested on many downstream tasks, including [translation](/index.php?title=Translation&action=edit&redlink=1), [summarisation](/index.php?title=Summarisation&action=edit&redlink=1), [question and answering](/index.php?title=Question_and_answering&action=edit&redlink=1), and [reading comprehension](/index.php?title=Reading_comprehension&action=edit&redlink=1).
