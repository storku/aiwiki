**Together AI** (formerly Together Computer) is an American [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) company that operates a cloud platform for building, training, fine-tuning, and deploying open-source [AI models](/index.php?title=AI_model&action=edit&redlink=1). Founded in June 2022 by Vipul Ved Prakash, Ce Zhang, [Chris Ré](/index.php?title=Chris_R%C3%A9&action=edit&redlink=1), and Percy Liang, the company is headquartered in San Francisco, California. Together AI is widely recognized for its contributions to the open-source AI ecosystem, most notably through the [RedPajama](/index.php?title=RedPajama&action=edit&redlink=1) dataset project, and for providing high-performance [inference](/index.php?title=Inference_(machine_learning)&action=edit&redlink=1) infrastructure that serves as an alternative to proprietary cloud providers. As of February 2025, Together AI is valued at $3.3 billion.

## History

### Founding and Early Development (2022)

Together AI was incorporated in June 2022 in Menlo Park, California, under the name Together Computer. The company was co-founded by four individuals who brought a combination of industry experience and deep academic expertise in [machine learning](/wiki/machine_learning) and data systems.

**Vipul Ved Prakash**, the company's CEO, previously founded the social media search platform Topsy, which was acquired by [Apple](/index.php?title=Apple_Inc.&action=edit&redlink=1) in 2013 for approximately $200 million. Following the acquisition, Prakash served as a senior director at Apple. His experience in building scalable data infrastructure proved instrumental in shaping Together AI's platform vision.

**Ce Zhang** is a professor of computer science, formerly at ETH Zurich and later at the University of Chicago. His research focuses on decentralized AI systems and data management for machine learning, areas that directly informed Together AI's approach to distributed computing for model training.

**[Chris Ré](/index.php?title=Chris_R%C3%A9&action=edit&redlink=1)** is a professor of computer science at Stanford University and a [MacArthur Fellowship](/index.php?title=MacArthur_Fellowship&action=edit&redlink=1) recipient. He co-founded several other AI startups, including [SambaNova Systems](/index.php?title=SambaNova_Systems&action=edit&redlink=1), which builds hardware and integrated systems for AI workloads. Ré's research on data-centric AI and foundation models has been influential across the industry.

**Percy Liang** is a computer science professor at Stanford and directs the university's Center for Research on Foundation Models (CRFM). His work on evaluating and benchmarking [large language models](/wiki/large_language_model) has helped shape standards for measuring model performance across the field.

The founding team's motivation was rooted in a shared concern that computing resources for AI model training were becoming increasingly concentrated among a small number of large technology companies. Together AI was created to democratize access to AI infrastructure and to champion the open-source model development ecosystem.

### Seed and Early Funding (2023)

In May 2023, Together AI raised $20 million in seed funding, establishing its initial foothold in the competitive AI infrastructure market. This early round attracted investors who saw potential in the company's vision of providing an open-source alternative to the proprietary AI clouds operated by major tech companies.

### RedPajama Project Launch (2023)

One of Together AI's most significant early contributions to the AI community came with the launch of the [RedPajama](/index.php?title=RedPajama&action=edit&redlink=1) dataset project in April 2023. This collaborative initiative, undertaken in partnership with institutions including Ontocord.ai, ETH DS3Lab, Stanford CRFM, and Hazy Research, aimed to reproduce the training dataset used for [Meta](/wiki/meta)'s [LLaMA](/wiki/llama) models. The LLaMA training corpus, described in Meta's research paper, had not been publicly released, which prevented researchers and smaller companies from transparently reproducing or building upon the model.

RedPajama-V1 (RedPajama-Data-1T) consisted of 1.2 trillion tokens and was released as a fully open dataset. The data was sourced from seven primary categories:

| Source | Tokens (Billions) | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CommonCrawl | 878 | Web crawl data filtered for quality |
| C4 | 175 | Cleaned version of Common Crawl |
| GitHub | 59 | Open-source code repositories |
| Wikipedia | 24 | English Wikipedia articles |
| Books | 26 | Public domain books |
| ArXiv | 28 | Scientific papers |
| StackExchange | 20 | Q&A discussions |

The release of RedPajama-V1 was a landmark moment for open-source AI, as it provided the research community with a transparent, reproducible training corpus comparable in scale to those used by leading proprietary models.

### Series A Funding (2023)

In November 2023, Together AI closed a $102.5 million Series A funding round led by [Kleiner Perkins](/index.php?title=Kleiner_Perkins&action=edit&redlink=1), with participation from [NVIDIA](/wiki/nvidia) and Emergence Capital. This round significantly expanded the company's resources for platform development and infrastructure buildout. A subsequent funding round led by [Salesforce](/index.php?title=Salesforce&action=edit&redlink=1) Ventures valued the company at $1.25 billion, establishing Together AI as a [unicorn](/index.php?title=Unicorn_(finance)&action=edit&redlink=1).

### RedPajama-V2 (2024)

In 2024, Together AI released RedPajama-V2, which expanded the dataset to approximately 30 trillion tokens, making it the largest publicly available open training corpus at the time. The dataset has been used in the training of several prominent production models, including Snowflake Arctic, Salesforce's XGen, and [AI2](/index.php?title=Allen_Institute_for_AI&action=edit&redlink=1)'s OLMo. The RedPajama project solidified Together AI's reputation as a key contributor to the open-source AI ecosystem and demonstrated the company's commitment to transparency in model development.

### Series B Funding and Growth (2025)

On February 20, 2025, Together AI announced a $305 million Series B investment led by General Catalyst and co-led by Prosperity7 (the venture arm of Saudi Aramco). This round valued the company at $3.3 billion and included plans for large-scale deployment of [NVIDIA](/wiki/nvidia) Blackwell GPUs.

By the second half of 2025, Together AI had established its own data centers to provide infrastructure for its growing customer base:

| Data Center | Location | Operational Since |
| --- | --- | --- |
| East Coast | Maryland, USA | July 2025 |
| Central | Memphis, Tennessee, USA | 2025 |
| Europe | Sweden | September 2025 |

The company secured 200 MW of power capacity across North America and began deploying NVIDIA Blackwell GPU clusters to meet the growing demand for AI training and inference workloads.

### 2026 Milestones

In March 2026, Together AI held its first AI Native Conference, where the company announced significant business and product milestones. By this point, the platform served thousands of enterprise customers and supported over one million developers. The company reported 10x year-over-year growth in annual contract revenue (ACR), with 27 customer deals exceeding $1 million and one deal exceeding $1 billion. Revenue estimates from Sacra placed Together AI at approximately $300 million in annualized revenue as of September 2025, up from $130 million at the end of 2024.

## Products and Services

Together AI's platform spans the entire AI model lifecycle, from training and fine-tuning to inference and deployment. The company differentiates itself by focusing on open-source models and providing infrastructure optimized for their specific requirements.

### Together Inference

Together Inference is the company's flagship product, enabling developers to deploy and run inference on a wide range of open-source AI models without managing the underlying server infrastructure. The service provides access to a library of over 200 open-source models for tasks including text generation, image generation, code completion, and embeddings.

By early 2026, Together AI's optimized [LLM](/wiki/large_language_model) infrastructure delivered inference 3x to 5x faster than typical cloud options, a competitive advantage achieved through custom kernel optimizations and hardware-software co-design. The platform supports models from major open-source families including:

| Model Family | Developer | Example Models |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [LLaMA](/wiki/llama) | [Meta](/wiki/meta) | Llama 3.1 8B, Llama 3.3 70B, Llama 4 |
| [Mistral](/index.php?title=Mistral_AI&action=edit&redlink=1) | Mistral AI | Mistral Small 3, Mistral 7B |
| [Qwen](/index.php?title=Qwen&action=edit&redlink=1) | [Alibaba](/index.php?title=Alibaba_Group&action=edit&redlink=1) | Qwen 2.5, Qwen3-235B |
| [DeepSeek](/index.php?title=DeepSeek&action=edit&redlink=1) | DeepSeek | DeepSeek-V3 |
| [Gemma](/index.php?title=Gemma_(model)&action=edit&redlink=1) | [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1) | Gemma 3 |

Together Inference supports function calling and tool use for compatible models, enabling integration with external services and agentic workflows.

### Together Fine-Tuning

The fine-tuning service allows developers to customize open-source models on their own data. Together AI supports several fine-tuning methods:

- **Full Fine-Tuning**: Adjusts all model weights for maximum customization.
- **LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation)**: Fine-tunes only a small subset of weights, offering faster training at lower cost. This method is recommended for most use cases.
- **Vision Fine-Tuning**: Allows training vision-language models directly on raw image data for models like [Llama 4](/index.php?title=Llama_4&action=edit&redlink=1), Qwen3-VL, and Gemma-3.

In September 2025, Together AI expanded its fine-tuning capabilities to support models with over 100 billion parameters (such as DeepSeek-V3 and Qwen3-235B), with up to 6x higher throughput compared to earlier versions. The platform also added native support for tool call training, reasoning model fine-tuning, and seamless integration with models hosted on [Hugging Face](/index.php?title=Hugging_Face&action=edit&redlink=1).

### Together GPU Clusters

For organizations requiring dedicated compute resources, Together AI offers GPU clusters that provide direct access to high-performance hardware for training custom models. These clusters utilize NVIDIA GPUs, including the latest Blackwell architecture, and are available through Together AI's own data centers in Maryland, Memphis, and Sweden.

### Agentic Workflows

Together AI's platform supports agentic workflows with built-in code interpretation capabilities. This feature allows developers to build AI agents that can execute multi-step tasks, interact with external tools, and maintain state across complex workflows.

### Synthetic Data Generation

The platform includes tools for generating synthetic training data, which can be used to augment existing datasets or create entirely new datasets for fine-tuning. This capability is particularly valuable for organizations with limited labeled data.

## RedPajama Dataset

The [RedPajama](/index.php?title=RedPajama&action=edit&redlink=1) dataset is one of Together AI's most significant contributions to the open-source AI ecosystem. The project originated as a collaborative effort to democratize access to high-quality training data for [large language models](/wiki/large_language_model).

### RedPajama-V1

Released on April 17, 2023, RedPajama-V1 was a 1.2 trillion token dataset created by following the recipe described in [Meta](/wiki/meta)'s [LLaMA](/wiki/llama) paper. The project was a collaboration between Together AI, Ontocord.ai, ETH DS3Lab, Stanford CRFM, and Hazy Research. The goal was to provide a fully open, reproducible alternative to the proprietary training data used by leading AI labs.

### RedPajama-V2

Released in 2024, RedPajama-V2 expanded the dataset to approximately 30 trillion tokens across more than 100 billion documents in five languages. It included quality annotations and metadata for each document, enabling researchers to filter and curate subsets tailored to specific use cases. A research paper describing the dataset was published on arXiv in November 2024.

### Impact

The RedPajama datasets have been widely adopted across the AI research community and industry. Notable models trained on RedPajama data include:
