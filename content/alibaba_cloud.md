Alibaba Cloud** (Chinese: 阿里云, also known as **Aliyun**) is the cloud computing arm of [Alibaba Group](/index.php?title=Alibaba_Group&action=edit&redlink=1), focused on [cloud computing](/index.php?title=Cloud_computing&action=edit&redlink=1) infrastructure and [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) (AI) platforms. Since 2023 it has become a prominent developer and operator of the **[Qwen](/wiki/qwen)** (Tongyi Qianwen*) family of [large language models](/wiki/large_language_model) (LLMs), alongside full-stack AI tooling such as the Machine Learning **[Platform for AI](/index.php?title=Platform_for_AI&action=edit&redlink=1) (PAI)**, **[Model Studio](/index.php?title=Model_Studio&action=edit&redlink=1)** for foundation-model APIs, and the **[ModelScope](/index.php?title=ModelScope&action=edit&redlink=1)** open-source model community.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-AR2024-1)[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-PAI_Overview-2)[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-ModelStudio-3)[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-ModelScopeLaunch-4)

## Overview

Alibaba Cloud provides public cloud services and AI platforms used within Alibaba's ecosystem and by external customers. It is the largest cloud computing company in China and the Asia-Pacific region according to Gartner reports.[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-gartner2023-5) Its AI stack spans [foundation model](/wiki/foundation_model) development (Qwen family), model hosting and inference (PAI-EAS), application-ready APIs (Model Studio), and an open community for model weights, datasets, and demos (ModelScope).[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-PAI_Overview-2)[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-ModelStudio-3)[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-AR2024-1)

As of 2025, the company operates in 29 regions with 87 availability zones worldwide.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-6) AI-related products have driven significant growth, contributing to a 26% year-over-year revenue increase in Q1 FY2026, with AI revenue maintaining triple-digit growth for eight consecutive quarters through 2025.[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-pymnts-ai-growth-7)[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-q1fy26-8)

In February 2025, Alibaba Group announced a US$53 billion investment over three years in AI and cloud infrastructure, marking one of the largest investments in AI development globally.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-investment-9) In September 2023, Alibaba Cloud gained Chinese regulatory approval to open its Tongyi Qianwen models to the public, marking a milestone in commercial generative AI availability in China.[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-Reuters_Public-10)

## History

### Foundation and Early Development (2009-2016)

- **2009**: Alibaba Cloud is founded in September to provide cloud infrastructure for Alibaba's platforms and external customers.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-6)

- **2010**: Introduces proprietary cloud computing engine [Apsara](/index.php?title=Apsara&action=edit&redlink=1), which becomes the foundation of Alibaba Cloud's services; supports Taobao's first Singles' Day event, managing 2.4 billion page views in 24 hours.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-timeline-11)

- **2012**: Becomes first Chinese cloud service provider to pass ISO 27001:2005 certification.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-6)

- **2013**: Merges with HiChina, acquiring the www.net.cn domain services business.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-6)

- **2014**: Hong Kong data center goes online; successfully defends against 14-hour DDoS attack peaking at 453.8 Gbit/s.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-6)

- **2015**: Alibaba Group invests US$1 billion in Alibaba Cloud; opens Singapore data center and designates it as overseas headquarters.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-6)

### Global Expansion and AI Development (2017-2022)

- **2017**: Becomes official cloud services partner of the Olympics; establishes [DAMO Academy](/index.php?title=DAMO_Academy&action=edit&redlink=1) (Discovery, Adventure, Momentum and Outlook) with $15 billion commitment for AI and technology research; placed in Gartner's Visionaries quadrant for Cloud IaaS.[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-damo-12)[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-6)

- **2019**: T-Head (Alibaba's semiconductor unit) unveils **[Hanguang 800](/index.php?title=Hanguang_800&action=edit&redlink=1)**, a data-center AI inference [ASIC](/index.php?title=Application-specific_integrated_circuit&action=edit&redlink=1) with 820 TOPS performance used to accelerate machine-learning workloads on Alibaba Cloud.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-HG800_Blog-13)[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-HG800_THead-14)

- **2021**: Opens Philippines data center; unveils in-house ARM-based Yitian 710 chip for data centers.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-6)

- **2022**: Launch of **[ModelScope](/index.php?title=ModelScope&action=edit&redlink=1)**, an open-source "Model-as-a-Service" (MaaS) platform hosting hundreds (later thousands) of models and datasets for developers and researchers; announces US$1 billion pledge to upgrade global partner ecosystem.[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-ModelScopeLaunch-4)[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-6)

### Generative AI Era (2023-Present)

- **2023**: Tongyi Qianwen ([Qwen](/wiki/qwen)) debuts in April and later receives regulatory clearance to go public in September; Alibaba Cloud begins releasing multiple model sizes (including open-weight variants).[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-Reuters_Public-10)[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-AR2024-1)[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-qwenlaunch-15)

- **2024-2025**: Expansion of the Qwen ecosystem (Qwen2/2.5 and [QwQ](/index.php?title=QwQ&action=edit&redlink=1) reasoning models; later [Qwen3](/index.php?title=Qwen3&action=edit&redlink=1) series), multimodal variants ([Qwen-VL](/index.php?title=Qwen-VL&action=edit&redlink=1)), and Model Studio APIs; AI-driven cloud revenue momentum reaches 26% growth in Q1 FY2026.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-Qwen_GitHub-16)[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-Bizwire_DecQ2024-17)

- **2025**: February announcement of US$53 billion investment over three years; September unveiling of [Qwen3-Max](/index.php?title=Qwen3-Max&action=edit&redlink=1) with over 1 trillion parameters; announcement of additional AI investments beyond initial commitment.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-investment-9)[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-Reuters_Qwen3Max-18)[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-cnbc-19)

### AI for Good Initiatives

#### Healthcare Applications

DAMO Academy has developed breakthrough AI-powered healthcare tools:

- **[PANDA](/index.php?title=PANDA&action=edit&redlink=1)** (Pancreatic Cancer Detection with Artificial Intelligence): Deep learning model detecting pancreatic lesions using non-contrast CT scans, achieving 92.9% sensitivity and 99.9% specificity[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-panda-21)

- **[GRAPE](/index.php?title=GRAPE&action=edit&redlink=1)** (Gastric Cancer Risk Assessment Procedure): Framework for analyzing 3D CT scans to detect gastric cancers

In 2024, Alibaba became the only Chinese company ranked in Fortune's top 10 "Change the World" list due to its AI-assisted cancer detection technology.[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-damomed-22)

#### Climate and Energy

DAMO Academy leverages AI for renewable energy forecasting, improving the reliability of solar and wind power predictions for grid operators across China.[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-energy-23)

## AI Platforms and Services

### [Platform for AI](/index.php?title=Platform_for_AI&action=edit&redlink=1) (PAI)

PAI is Alibaba Cloud's comprehensive end-to-end machine-learning platform providing data processing, training, and deployment. It supports large-scale training (tens of billions of features / hundreds of billions of samples) and integrates **[Elastic Algorithm Service](/index.php?title=Elastic_Algorithm_Service&action=edit&redlink=1) (EAS)** for one-click online inference with elastic scaling and monitoring.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-PAI_Overview-2)[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-EAS_Overview-24)[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-EAS_Deploy-25)

Sub-products include:

- **[PAI-iTAG](/index.php?title=PAI-iTAG&action=edit&redlink=1)**: Multimodal data labeling for efficient data preparation

- **[PAI-DSW](/index.php?title=PAI-DSW&action=edit&redlink=1)** (Data Science Workshop): Interactive programming tool with Jupyter Notebook support, optimized TensorFlow, and open-source frameworks for model building; supports CPU/GPU hybrid scheduling

- **[PAI-Designer](/index.php?title=PAI-Designer&action=edit&redlink=1)**: Codeless development tool with drag-and-drop components for classic ML algorithms

- **[PAI-DLC](/index.php?title=PAI-DLC&action=edit&redlink=1)** (Deep Learning Containers): Cloud-native deep learning platform for training, compatible with predefined and custom frameworks

- **[PAI-EAS](/index.php?title=PAI-EAS&action=edit&redlink=1)** (Elastic Algorithm Service): Model deployment tool for inference, enabling one-click deployment, scaling, and online debugging of complex models

- **[PAI-Blade](/index.php?title=PAI-Blade&action=edit&redlink=1)**: Universal optimization for inference performance

The platform features over 140 built-in optimization algorithms and supports popular frameworks including [TensorFlow](/wiki/tensorflow), [PyTorch](/index.php?title=PyTorch&action=edit&redlink=1), [Megatron](/index.php?title=Megatron&action=edit&redlink=1), and [DeepSpeed](/index.php?title=DeepSpeed&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-official-pai-26) In 2025, PAI-EAS introduced distributed inference capabilities with multi-node architecture, achieving a 92% increase in concurrency and 91% increase in tokens per second with Qwen2.5-72B model.[&#91;27&#93;](#cite_note-cwupgrade-27)

### Model Studio (Foundation-Model APIs)

**[Model Studio](/index.php?title=Model_Studio&action=edit&redlink=1)** is a managed GenAI platform that exposes Alibaba's latest foundation models, such as [Qwen](/wiki/qwen) (text), [Qwen-VL](/index.php?title=Qwen-VL&action=edit&redlink=1) (vision-language), and [Wan](/index.php?title=Wan&action=edit&redlink=1) (image/video generation), via REST APIs and SDKs, with key management, usage metering, and enterprise controls.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-ModelStudio-3)[&#91;28&#93;](#cite_note-ModelStudio_GetStarted-28)

Features include:

- Access to [Qwen](/wiki/qwen) series models including [Qwen-Max](/index.php?title=Qwen-Max&action=edit&redlink=1), [Qwen-Plus](/index.php?title=Qwen-Plus&action=edit&redlink=1), and [Qwen-Turbo](/index.php?title=Qwen-Turbo&action=edit&redlink=1)

- [Supervised fine-tuning](/wiki/supervised_fine-tuning) (SFT) and [low-rank adaptation](/wiki/low-rank_adaptation) (LoRA)

- Model compression and inference acceleration

- One-click [RAG](/index.php?title=RAG&action=edit&redlink=1) with [AnalyticDB](/index.php?title=AnalyticDB&action=edit&redlink=1) (supporting 10 billion vectors)

- OpenAI-compatible API interfaces

- Secure deployment in Virtual Private Cloud (VPC) environments

Alibaba Cloud has also offered serverless options for PAI-EAS and promoted Model Studio for regulated industries via private-cloud deployment options.[&#91;29&#93;](#cite_note-AV_Serverless-29)[&#91;30&#93;](#cite_note-TechMag_Private-30)

### ModelScope (Open-Source AI Hub)

Launched at Apsara 2022, **[ModelScope](/index.php?title=ModelScope&action=edit&redlink=1)** is Alibaba Cloud's open-source hub where developers can browse and run models (including open-weight Qwen variants) and access datasets and demos. The platform provides:

- Over 300 ready-to-deploy AI models

- 150+ state-of-the-art (SOTA) models

- Support for [computer vision](/wiki/computer_vision), [natural language processing](/index.php?title=Natural_language_processing&action=edit&redlink=1), and audio tasks

- Free online testing and deployment capabilities

- Integration with Alibaba Cloud computing resources

In 2024 Alibaba expanded ModelScope's English-language access for international users.[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-ModelScopeLaunch-4)[&#91;31&#93;](#cite_note-ModelScope_EN-31)[&#91;32&#93;](#cite_note-AIBusiness_ModelScope-32)

## Qwen (Tongyi Qianwen) Model Family

**[Qwen](/wiki/qwen)** (Chinese: 通义千问, meaning "universal truth" or "general meaning") is Alibaba Cloud's flagship LLM family. Initial public availability followed Chinese regulatory approval in September 2023, with subsequent open-weight releases of several sizes. The family expanded through 2024–2025 ([Qwen2](/index.php?title=Qwen2&action=edit&redlink=1)/2.5, reasoning-optimized [QwQ](/index.php?title=QwQ&action=edit&redlink=1), multimodal [Qwen-VL](/index.php?title=Qwen-VL&action=edit&redlink=1), and **[Qwen3](/index.php?title=Qwen3&action=edit&redlink=1)** models with both dense and Mixture-of-Experts variants).[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-Reuters_Public-10)[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-Qwen_GitHub-16)

The models have been trained on over 36 trillion tokens in 119 languages and dialects, achieving notable [benchmarks](/wiki/benchmarks), ranking as the top Chinese language model and third globally behind models from [Anthropic](/wiki/anthropic) and [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) as of July 2024.[&#91;33&#93;](#cite_note-qwenwiki-33) Models have been downloaded over 40 million times and are available through [Hugging Face](/wiki/hugging_face), [GitHub](/index.php?title=GitHub&action=edit&redlink=1), and ModelScope.[&#91;33&#93;](#cite_note-qwenwiki-33)

### Adoption and Pricing

By mid-2024 Alibaba said Qwen models had more than 90,000 enterprise clients across sectors such as smartphones and gaming, reflecting rapid uptake in China's generative-AI market.[&#91;38&#93;](#cite_note-WSJ_90k-38) In May 2024 Chinese tech firms, including Alibaba, cut LLM API prices substantially; Alibaba reduced some Qwen prices by up to 97% as competition intensified.[&#91;39&#93;](#cite_note-Reuters_PriceCuts-39)
