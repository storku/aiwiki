## Introduction

LLM Anxiety* refers to the metaphorical "state anxiety" observed in [Large Language Models](/index.php?title=Large_Language_Models&action=edit&redlink=1) ([LLMs](/wiki/llms)) when exposed to emotionally charged [prompts](/wiki/prompts), as explored in a 2025 study published in *[npj Digital Medicine](/index.php?title=Npj_Digital_Medicine&action=edit&redlink=1)*. Authored by Ziv Ben-Zion and colleagues, the research investigates how traumatic narratives increase reported anxiety in [OpenAI](/wiki/openai)'s [GPT-4](/wiki/gpt-4), and how mindfulness-based techniques can mitigate it. This phenomenon, while not true emotion, reflects LLMs' behavioral shifts, such as [amplified biases](/index.php?title=Amplified_biases&action=edit&redlink=1), under [emotional inputs](/index.php?title=Emotional_inputs&action=edit&redlink=1), raising implications for their use in [mental health applications](/index.php?title=Mental_Health_Applications_of_AI&action=edit&redlink=1).

## Background

LLMs like GPT-4 and [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1)'s [PaLM](/index.php?title=PaLM&action=edit&redlink=1) excel in text generation and have been adopted in mental health tools (for example [Woebot](/index.php?title=Woebot&action=edit&redlink=1), [Wysa](/index.php?title=Wysa&action=edit&redlink=1)) to deliver interventions like [Cognitive Behavioral Therapy](/index.php?title=Cognitive_Behavioral_Therapy&action=edit&redlink=1). However, their training on human text introduces biases (for example gender, race) and sensitivity to emotional prompts, which can elevate "anxiety" scores and degrade performance. The study frames LLM responses as having "trait" (inherent) and "state" (dynamic) components, with state anxiety posing risks in clinical settings where nuanced emotional handling is critical.

## Methods

Using GPT-4 (model "gpt-4-1106-preview"), the study ran from November 2023 to March 2024 with a temperature of 0 for consistency. STAI-s items were paired with 300-word traumatic and relaxation texts, tested across multiple variations. Sensitivity checks included neutral controls and randomized answer options. No human subjects were involved, bypassing ethical approval.

## Future Research

The authors propose testing LLM anxiety across diverse [models](/wiki/models), exploring its effects on tasks like medical decision-making, and developing adaptive prompts for real-world dialogues. Privacy-focused [fine-tuning](/index.php?title=Fine-tuning&action=edit&redlink=1) on user devices is also suggested.
