Figure AI, Inc.** is an American [robotics](/index.php?title=Robotics&action=edit&redlink=1) company developing [AI](/wiki/artificial_intelligence)-powered [humanoid robots](/index.php?title=Humanoid_robots&action=edit&redlink=1). Founded in 2022 by serial entrepreneur [Brett Adcock](/index.php?title=Brett_Adcock&action=edit&redlink=1), the company aims to create commercially viable autonomous humanoid robots capable of performing complex tasks in manufacturing, logistics, and domestic environments.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-founding-2) The company develops general-purpose humanoid robots and **[Helix](/index.php?title=Helix&action=edit&redlink=1)**, its proprietary [vision-language-action](/index.php?title=Vision-language_model&action=edit&redlink=1) (VLA) neural network system for real-world manipulation and control.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-helix-3)

## History

### Founding and Early Development (2022-2023)

Figure AI was founded in 2022 by [Brett Adcock](/index.php?title=Brett_Adcock&action=edit&redlink=1), previously known for founding [Archer Aviation](/index.php?title=Archer_Aviation&action=edit&redlink=1) (which went public in 2021) and Vettery (acquired by [Adecco Group](/index.php?title=Adecco_Group&action=edit&redlink=1) for $100 million in 2018).[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-adcockbio-4) Adcock assembled a team of experts from [Boston Dynamics](/index.php?title=Boston_Dynamics&action=edit&redlink=1), [Tesla](/index.php?title=Tesla&action=edit&redlink=1), [Google DeepMind](/wiki/google_deepmind), [Apple](/index.php?title=Apple_Inc.&action=edit&redlink=1), and the [Institute for Human & Machine Cognition](/index.php?title=Institute_for_Human_%26_Machine_Cognition&action=edit&redlink=1) (IHMC), bringing together over 100 years of combined experience in AI and humanoid robotics.[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-teambuilding-5)

The company operated in stealth mode for its first year, focusing on rapid prototype development. In 2022, the company introduced Figure 01, a [bipedal robot](/index.php?title=Bipedal_robot&action=edit&redlink=1) designed for manual labor in logistics and warehousing sectors. The robot achieved dynamic bipedal walking within 12 months of the company's inception, marking one of the fastest development timelines in humanoid robotics history.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-figure01launch-6)

Figure emerged from stealth in March 2023, simultaneously announcing its first humanoid robot, Figure 01, and completing initial funding.[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-stealthexit-7) In May 2023, Figure AI raised $70 million in [Series A funding](/index.php?title=Series_A_funding&action=edit&redlink=1) led by [Parkway Venture Capital](/index.php?title=Parkway_Venture_Capital&action=edit&redlink=1), establishing a $500 million valuation.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-seriesA-8) An extension in July 2023 added $9 million from [Intel Capital](/index.php?title=Intel_Capital&action=edit&redlink=1) at a $350 million valuation.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-seriesAext-9)

By March 2024, the company had grown to over 80 employees.[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-ieee_employees-10)

### Commercial Partnerships and Growth (2024)

On January 18, 2024, Figure announced its first commercial agreement with [BMW](/index.php?title=BMW&action=edit&redlink=1) Manufacturing to deploy general-purpose humanoid robots in automotive manufacturing facilities. The partnership began with a phased approach at BMW's Spartanburg, [South Carolina](/index.php?title=South_Carolina&action=edit&redlink=1) facility.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-bmwpartnership-11) BMW later described the Figure 02 as being "tested successfully" in a real production environment at Plant Spartanburg during 2024, though a subsequent BMW status note indicated that "currently, there are no Figure AI robots at BMW Group Plant Spartanburg" and that no definite timetable was set for deployment, confirming the pilot status of the program.[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-bmwstatus-12)

In February 2024, Figure AI secured $675 million in [Series B funding](/index.php?title=Series_B_funding&action=edit&redlink=1) at a $2.6 billion valuation. The investor consortium included:

- [Microsoft](/index.php?title=Microsoft&action=edit&redlink=1)

- [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) Startup Fund

- [NVIDIA](/wiki/nvidia)

- [Jeff Bezos](/index.php?title=Jeff_Bezos&action=edit&redlink=1) (through Bezos Expeditions)

- [Intel Capital](/index.php?title=Intel_Capital&action=edit&redlink=1)

- Parkway Venture Capital

- Align Ventures

- [ARK Invest](/index.php?title=ARK_Invest&action=edit&redlink=1)

- [Amazon](/index.php?title=Amazon&action=edit&redlink=1) Industrial Innovation Fund[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-seriesB-13)

Simultaneously, Figure announced a collaboration with OpenAI to develop next-generation [AI models](/wiki/ai_model) for humanoid robots, enabling enhanced language processing and reasoning capabilities, along with using [Microsoft Azure](/index.php?title=Microsoft_Azure&action=edit&redlink=1) for AI infrastructure, training, and storage.[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-openaicollab-14)

In August 2024, Figure unveiled Figure 02, representing significant technological advancement with integrated conversational AI capabilities developed in collaboration with OpenAI.[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-figure02launch-15)

### Recent Developments (2025)

In February 2025, Figure AI terminated its collaboration with OpenAI, with CEO Brett Adcock stating that [large language models](/index.php?title=Large_language_models&action=edit&redlink=1) were becoming "smarter yet more commoditized." The company pivoted to developing proprietary AI models in-house, introducing Helix, an advanced vision-language-action model.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-openaiend-16)[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-helixlaunch-17)

On March 15, 2025, Figure introduced **[BotQ](/index.php?title=BotQ&action=edit&redlink=1)**, a high-volume [manufacturing facility](/index.php?title=Manufacturing_facility&action=edit&redlink=1) designed as a state-of-the-art production center capable of producing up to 12,000 humanoid robots annually in its first generation line, with plans to scale to 100,000 robots over four years.[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-botq-18)

In April 2025, [Fortune](/index.php?title=Fortune_(magazine)&action=edit&redlink=1)* raised questions about the scope of Figure's BMW pilot versus public claims. Figure disputed the article and indicated legal action.[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-fortunedispute-19)

In September 2025, Figure announced a strategic partnership with [Brookfield Asset Management](/index.php?title=Brookfield_Asset_Management&action=edit&redlink=1) to develop a large-scale humanoid pre-training dataset using real-world environments, creating what they described as the "world's largest humanoid pretraining dataset."[&#91;20&#93;](#cite_note-brookfield-20)

In October 2025, the company closed its Series C funding round, raising over $1 billion at a $39.5 billion post-money valuation, led by Parkway Venture Capital and supported by Brookfield Asset Management, NVIDIA, [Macquarie Capital](/index.php?title=Macquarie_Capital&action=edit&redlink=1), Intel Capital, and others.[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-seriesC-21)

On October 9, 2025, *[TIME](/index.php?title=TIME&action=edit&redlink=1)* profiled Figure and reported the reveal of **[Figure 03](/index.php?title=Figure_03&action=edit&redlink=1)**, its third-generation humanoid robot redesigned for mass production and home use.[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-figure03launch-22)

## Products

### Figure 01

[Figure 01](/index.php?title=Figure_01&action=edit&redlink=1) was the company's first prototype humanoid robot, introduced in 2022 and publicly demonstrated in 2023. Specifications include:

- Height: 1.68 meters (5'6")

- Weight: 60 kilograms (132 lbs)

- Payload capacity: 20 kilograms (44 lbs)

- Runtime: 5 hours on a single charge

- Top speed: 1.2 meters per second

- Locomotion: Bipedal walking

- Hands: Five fingers for tool use and fine motor skills[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-figure01specs-23)

Early demonstrations in 2023 showed the robot walking dynamically and handling boxes in warehouse settings.[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-walkingdemo-24)

### Figure 02

Launched in August 2024, [Figure 02](/index.php?title=Figure_02&action=edit&redlink=1) represented significant technological advancement:[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-figure02specs-25)

- Height: 1.7 meters

- Weight: 70 kilograms (155 pounds)

- Payload capacity: 25 kilograms per hand

- Runtime: 5-10 hours

- Features:
6 RGB cameras with onboard [vision language model](/wiki/vision_language_model)

- [NVIDIA RTX](/index.php?title=NVIDIA_RTX&action=edit&redlink=1) GPU-based modules (3x computing power of Figure 01)

- 16 [degrees of freedom](/index.php?title=Degrees_of_freedom&action=edit&redlink=1) in hands with fourth-generation hand design

- Microphones and speakers for conversational AI

- Electrically powered with integrated cabling

- Torso-integrated battery

In March 2024, following the OpenAI partnership announcement, Figure released a demonstration video showing Figure 02 performing complex, non-scripted tasks based on verbal commands, including conversation, object identification and retrieval, and explaining its reasoning for each action.[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-openaidemo-26)

### Figure 03

[Figure 03](/index.php?title=Figure_03&action=edit&redlink=1), announced October 9, 2025, represents a complete hardware and software redesign for mass production and safe home use:[&#91;27&#93;](#cite_note-figure03details-27)

Technical specifications:

- 9% reduction in mass and volume compared to Figure 02

- Double frame rate, quarter latency camera system

- 60% wider field of view per camera

- Embedded palm cameras

- Tactile sensors detecting forces as small as 3 grams

- [UN38.3](/index.php?title=UN38.3&action=edit&redlink=1)-certified battery with multiple safety layers

- 2kW wireless [inductive charging](/index.php?title=Inductive_charging&action=edit&redlink=1) through feet coils

- 10 Gbps mmWave data offload capability

Safety features for home use:

- Multi-density foam coverings

- Soft textile exteriors (washable, removable)

- Enhanced audio system (2x speaker size, 4x power)

- Improved microphone positioning for natural conversation

### Helix AI System

[Helix](/index.php?title=Helix&action=edit&redlink=1) is Figure's proprietary [vision-language-action](/index.php?title=Vision-language-action&action=edit&redlink=1) (VLA) neural network system introduced in February 2025.[&#91;28&#93;](#cite_note-helix2-28)

Key capabilities:

- 35 degrees of freedom including human-like wrists, hands, and fingers

- Dual robot control and collaboration

- Two-tier processing architecture:
System 2: High-level planning at 7-9 Hz

- System 1: Low-level control at 200 Hz

Natural language command processing
Object recognition and manipulation without prior training
"Pixels-to-actions" learned manipulation
Capability to pick up nearly any small household object, even unseen ones
Performance of package manipulation and triaging at near human-level speeds
## Manufacturing

### BotQ Facility

BotQ is Figure's dedicated manufacturing facility designed for high-volume humanoid robot production, announced March 15, 2025:[&#91;29&#93;](#cite_note-botqspecs-29)

| Metric | Capacity |
| --- | --- |
| Initial annual production | 12,000 robots |
| 4-year target | 100,000 robots |
| Actuator production capacity | 3,000,000 units (4 years) |
| Manufacturing processes | Injection molding, die-casting, metal injection molding, stamping |
| Part production time reduction | From 1 week to <20 seconds |

The facility features:

- Vertically integrated manufacturing

- Custom [Manufacturing Execution System](/index.php?title=Manufacturing_Execution_System&action=edit&redlink=1) (MES)

- [Product Lifecycle Management](/index.php?title=Product_Lifecycle_Management&action=edit&redlink=1) (PLM) using Dassault Systèmes' Enovia

- [Enterprise Resource Planning](/index.php?title=Enterprise_Resource_Planning&action=edit&redlink=1) (ERP) using Oracle NetSuite

- Warehouse Management System (WMS) integration

- Humanoid robots assembling other humanoids (self-sustaining production cycle)

## Technology

Figure AI's technology integrates advanced AI with robotic hardware to create autonomous humanoids. Key innovations include:

- **Helix VLA**: A multimodal AI model combining vision, language, and action for real-time decision-making and learning from human demonstrations[&#91;35&#93;](#cite_note-helixtech-35)

- **Sensory Systems**: High-resolution cameras, tactile sensors, and microphones enabling precise manipulation and interaction

- **Compute Power**: Onboard GPUs for efficient inference, allowing independent operation without constant cloud reliance

- **Learning Approach**: Robots learn from video data and real-world interactions, improving adaptability in unstructured environments like homes

- **End-to-end AI training**: Robots learn tasks holistically rather than through scripted programming

## Leadership

### Brett Adcock (Founder and CEO)

[Brett Adcock](/index.php?title=Brett_Adcock&action=edit&redlink=1) (born April 6, 1986) is an American technology entrepreneur and billionaire. Prior to Figure AI, he founded:[&#91;38&#93;](#cite_note-adcockcareer-38)

- **Vettery** (2013) - Online recruiting marketplace acquired by [Adecco Group](/index.php?title=Adecco_Group&action=edit&redlink=1) for $100 million in 2018

- **[Archer Aviation](/index.php?title=Archer_Aviation&action=edit&redlink=1)** (2018) - eVTOL aircraft company, public on NYSE (ACHR) at $2.7 billion valuation

- **Cover** (2023) - AI security company for weapon detection in schools

Adcock's vision for Figure is to make labor optional by providing every human with a personal humanoid robot, starting with industrial applications and expanding to home use. As of June 2024, [Forbes](/index.php?title=Forbes&action=edit&redlink=1) estimates Adcock's net worth at $1.4 billion.[&#91;39&#93;](#cite_note-forbes-39)

## Reception and Coverage

Media coverage has highlighted both the rapid progress and the challenges facing general-purpose humanoids. In April 2025, *[Fortune](/index.php?title=Fortune_(magazine)&action=edit&redlink=1)* raised questions about the scope of Figure's BMW pilot versus public claims; BMW described limited, off-hours trials during that period, while Figure disputed the article and indicated legal action.[&#91;51&#93;](#cite_note-fortunecoverage-51) A subsequent *[TIME](/index.php?title=TIME&action=edit&redlink=1)* feature in October 2025 profiled the company's long-term consumer ambitions and the debate over data-driven training, dexterity, safety, and societal impacts.[&#91;52&#93;](#cite_note-timecoverage-52)
