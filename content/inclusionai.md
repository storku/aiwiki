inclusionAI** is an [artificial general intelligence](/wiki/artificial_general_intelligence) (AGI) research initiative established by [Ant Group](/index.php?title=Ant_Group&action=edit&redlink=1), focused on developing and [open-sourcing](/index.php?title=Open-source&action=edit&redlink=1) advanced [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) systems.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-github-1) The initiative represents Ant Group's dedicated effort to work towards [AGI](/wiki/agi) through the development of [Large Language Models](/index.php?title=Large_Language_Models&action=edit&redlink=1) (LLMs), [Reinforcement Learning](/index.php?title=Reinforcement_Learning&action=edit&redlink=1) (RL) systems, multimodal models, and other AI-related frameworks and applications.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-huggingface-2) inclusionAI describes itself as a hub for open projects from Ant Group's research teams working toward reproducible and community-driven AI systems, with a stated mission to develop a fully open-sourced AI ecosystem.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-iclr-expo-3)

## Overview

inclusionAI operates as the primary vehicle for [Ant Group](/index.php?title=Ant_Group&action=edit&redlink=1)'s artificial general intelligence ambitions, maintaining a strong commitment to [open-source](/index.php?title=Open-source_software&action=edit&redlink=1) principles and collaborative development.[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-website-4) The organization develops and releases various AI models and tools designed to advance the field of AGI while ensuring accessibility and inclusivity in AI development.[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-sustainability-5)

The initiative is guided by principles of fairness, transparency, and collaboration, with a focus on tools for training and evaluating reasoning-oriented LLMs via RL, agent frameworks, and the release of trained model checkpoints when feasible.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-github-1) This aligns with Ant Group's broader "AI First" corporate strategy announced in 2024.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-recruitment-6) Public materials indicate that inclusionAI maintains repositories on [GitHub](/index.php?title=GitHub&action=edit&redlink=1) and model artifacts on [Hugging Face](/wiki/hugging_face), and has presented work at venues such as [ICLR 2025 Expo](/index.php?title=ICLR&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-iclr-expo-3)

## History

inclusionAI emerged as part of [Ant Group](/index.php?title=Ant_Group&action=edit&redlink=1)'s increased focus on artificial intelligence research and development. The initiative became prominently active in 2024-2025 with the release of multiple open-source models and frameworks.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-github-1) This move aligned with Ant Group's "Plan A" recruitment initiative, launched in April 2025, which aimed to attract top AI talents and ramp up innovation efforts.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-recruitment-6)[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-techinasia-hiring-7)

By May 2025, Ant Group publicly showcased its elite AI researchers, including figures like He Zhengyu, a PhD graduate from the Georgia Institute of Technology known for developing advanced algorithms.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-scmp-researchers-8) Public references to inclusionAI as a named project appear in 2025 in connection with an ICLR Expo session highlighting its open RL training stack and agent work.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-iclr-expo-3)

In March 2025, Ant Group announced the open-sourcing of the Ling [Mixture of Experts](/wiki/mixture_of_experts) (MoE) Large Language Models under the inclusionAI umbrella, marking a significant milestone in the initiative's development.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-ling-release-9) This was followed by the release of Ling-Plus and Ling-Lite models, which demonstrated the ability to train large-scale models on domestically produced Chinese chips from [Alibaba](/index.php?title=Alibaba_Group&action=edit&redlink=1) and [Huawei](/index.php?title=Huawei&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-chinese-chips-10)

inclusionAI's projects began appearing on platforms like GitHub and Hugging Face in mid-to-late 2025, with releases such as the Inclusion Arena leaderboard in August 2025.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-venturebeat-arena-11) In September 2025, the organization began open-sourcing Ling 2.0, a series of MoE architecture LLMs, with Ling-mini-2.0 as the first released version.[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-ling-mini-12) On September 30, 2025, the organization released Ring-1T-preview, a trillion-parameter reasoning model.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-ring-1t-13)

#### Ring-1T-preview

[Ring-1T-preview](/index.php?title=Ring-1T-preview&action=edit&redlink=1) is a preview checkpoint of a trillion-parameter "thinking" model released in late September 2025 on [Hugging Face](/wiki/hugging_face).[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-ring-hf-17) The model features a MoE architecture and was positioned to facilitate early community exploration. It excels in natural language reasoning and was trained on 20 trillion tokens, achieving 92.6% on the AIME 2025 (American Invitational Mathematics Examination) math benchmark.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-ring-1t-13) The model is optimized for tasks requiring deep thinking and long-term planning, such as code generation and complex problem-solving, and supports long-horizon problem solving.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-ring-1t-13) 

The model was fine-tuned using inclusionAI's custom RLVR framework with the icepop method.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-ring-1t-13) FP8 variants and community quantizations appeared shortly after on Hugging Face.[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-ring-fp8-18)[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-hf-quant-index-19) Third-party coverage reported Ring-1T-preview as the first open-source trillion-parameter model.[&#91;20&#93;](#cite_note-tia-ring-20)

#### Ming-Omni

[Ming-Omni](/index.php?title=Ming-Omni&action=edit&redlink=1) is an advanced open-source multimodal model capable of processing images, text, audio, and video, released in 2025.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-ming-omni-16) The model features a comprehensive multimodal processing architecture with MoE design and modality-specific routers.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-ming-omni-16) It supports speech and image generation, dialect understanding, voice cloning, context-aware dialogues, text-to-speech, and image editing.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-ming-omni-16) 

Ming-Omni represents a breakthrough in multimodal AI, integrating dedicated encoders for different modalities. It supports a wide range of tasks without additional fine-tuning, including generating natural speech, high-quality images, and handling dialect-specific interactions.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-ming-omni-16) The model has been described as the first open-source model matching GPT-4o's modality support, with all code and weights publicly available.[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-ming-omni-project-21)

### Frameworks and Tools

inclusionAI has developed several frameworks to support AGI research and development:

#### AReaL (Ant Reasoning RL)

[AReaL](/index.php?title=AReaL&action=edit&redlink=1) is an open-source, fully asynchronous [reinforcement learning](/wiki/reinforcement_learning) training system designed for large reasoning and agentic models.[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-areal-22) It decouples generation from training to improve GPU utilization and training stability, and provides details intended for full reproducibility (data, infra, and models).[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-areal-22)[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-areal-arxiv-23) The system emphasizes lightning-fast, efficient operations for training large-scale models, and was developed by the AReaL Team at Ant Group in collaboration with Tsinghua University's Institute for Interdisciplinary Information Sciences.[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-areal-22)

#### ASearcher

[ASearcher](/index.php?title=ASearcher&action=edit&redlink=1) is an open-source framework for large-scale online RL training of search agents, aiming to advance "Search Intelligence" to expert-level performance.[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-asearcher-24) The framework offers guidance to build customized agents, including integration with AReaL.[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-asearcher-24)

#### AWorld

[AWorld](/index.php?title=AWorld&action=edit&redlink=1) is a runtime system for building, evaluating and training general multi-agent assistance.[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-aworld-25) The system provides infrastructure for developing collaborative agent systems and testing their performance in various scenarios.[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-aworld-25)

#### Inclusion Arena

[Inclusion Arena](/index.php?title=Inclusion_Arena&action=edit&redlink=1) is a live leaderboard and open platform for evaluating large foundation models based on real-world, in-production applications, launched in August 2025.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-venturebeat-arena-11) The platform bridges AI-powered apps with state-of-the-art LLMs and multimodal LLMs (MLLMs).[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-venturebeat-arena-11) 

Unlike traditional lab-based benchmarks, Inclusion Arena prioritizes evaluations based on production environments to better reflect practical utility and addresses gaps in conventional evaluation methods by using production data.[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-inclusion-arena-arxiv-26) The platform was proposed by researchers from inclusionAI and Ant Group and shifts the paradigm of model evaluation from synthetic lab benchmarks to real-world performance metrics derived from production applications.[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-inclusion-arena-arxiv-26) The platform is live and open, inviting contributions from the AI community.[&#91;27&#93;](#cite_note-devx-arena-27)

#### ABench

[ABench](/index.php?title=ABench&action=edit&redlink=1) is a benchmark suite for evaluating AI models developed by inclusionAI.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-github-1)

## Technical Approach and Innovations

### Open, Reproducible Systems

inclusionAI's work emphasizes (i) open, reproducible RL training pipelines for reasoning-centric LLMs; (ii) asynchronous system designs that reduce training latency bottlenecks by decoupling rollout generation from parameter updates; and (iii) releasing code, data notes, and, when feasible, model weights for community use and inspection.[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-areal-22)[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-areal-arxiv-23)[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-github-1)

### Cost-Efficient Training

inclusionAI has pioneered methods for training large-scale models on resource-constrained hardware. The organization reported training costs of approximately $880,000 for their Ling models, representing a 20% cost reduction compared to traditional approaches.[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-chinese-chips-10) This was achieved through:

- Use of domestically produced Chinese chips from Alibaba and Huawei[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-ling-release-9)

- Implementation of the EDiT (Elastic Distributed Training) method[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-arxiv-14)

- FP8 mixed-precision training throughout the entire process[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-ling-mini-12)

- Novel optimization techniques for heterogeneous computing environments[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-arxiv-14)

### Open Source Commitment

All major models and frameworks developed by inclusionAI are released as [open-source software](/index.php?title=Open-source_software&action=edit&redlink=1), available through platforms including:

- [Hugging Face](/wiki/hugging_face) (model distribution)[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-huggingface-2)

## Research Focus Areas

inclusionAI's research spans multiple domains critical to AGI development:

- [Natural language processing](/index.php?title=Natural_language_processing&action=edit&redlink=1) and understanding

- [Reinforcement learning](/wiki/reinforcement_learning) for reasoning and agent systems

- [Multimodal AI](/index.php?title=Multimodal_learning&action=edit&redlink=1) combining vision, language, and speech

- Efficient training methods for resource-constrained environments

- Agent-based systems and multi-agent coordination

- Real-world evaluation and benchmarking

## Relationship to Ant Group and Ecosystem

inclusionAI sits within the wider [Ant Group](/index.php?title=Ant_Group&action=edit&redlink=1) technology and open-source ecosystem, which spans databases, privacy computing, and AI infrastructure. As part of Ant Group, inclusionAI's work supports the parent company's broader AI initiatives, including:

- Healthcare AI applications through the AQ app[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-sustainability-5)

- Financial services AI solutions[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-ling-release-9)

- Integration with [Alipay](/index.php?title=Alipay&action=edit&redlink=1) and other Ant Group services[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-ling-mini-12)

The models developed by inclusionAI are planned for use in industrial AI solutions across healthcare, finance, and other sectors served by Ant Group.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-ling-release-9) Ant Group communicates its open-source and research activities through corporate channels and events such as the INCLUSION·Conference on the Bund in [Shanghai](/index.php?title=Shanghai&action=edit&redlink=1), where it shares AI initiatives and related reports.[&#91;28&#93;](#cite_note-ant-tech-28)[&#91;29&#93;](#cite_note-inclusion-conf-29)

In September 2025, at the INCLUSION·Conference on the Bund, Ant Group highlighted its AI advancements, including open-source contributions from inclusionAI, underscoring the initiative's role in promoting trustworthy AI across industries.[&#91;30&#93;](#cite_note-aibase-conf-30)
