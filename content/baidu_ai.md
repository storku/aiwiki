Baidu AI** is the [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) division of [Baidu, Inc.](/index.php?title=Baidu&action=edit&redlink=1), a leading Chinese [technology company](/index.php?title=Technology_company&action=edit&redlink=1). The division encompasses Baidu's comprehensive AI research, development, and commercialization efforts, including [foundation models](/wiki/foundation_models), [autonomous driving](/index.php?title=Autonomous_driving&action=edit&redlink=1), [conversational AI](/index.php?title=Conversational_AI&action=edit&redlink=1), and [cloud computing](/index.php?title=Cloud_computing&action=edit&redlink=1) services. Baidu AI operates through the Artificial Intelligence Group (AIG) and provides AI technologies that power hundreds of millions of daily interactions across Baidu's ecosystem.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-baidu_wikipedia-1)

## Overview

Baidu AI spans fundamental research, platforms, and applied products across multiple domains:

- **Foundation models & Generative AI:** The [ERNIE](/index.php?title=ERNIE&action=edit&redlink=1) (Enhanced Representation through Knowledge Integration) series are [transformer](/index.php?title=Transformer_(machine_learning_model)&action=edit&redlink=1)-based [foundation models](/wiki/foundation_models). The public-facing chatbot [Ernie Bot](/index.php?title=Ernie_Bot&action=edit&redlink=1) (Wenxin Yiyan) reached over 200 million users by April 2024 and was made free to use from April 1, 2025.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-erniebot_wiki-2)[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-ernie45_launch-3)

- **AI Cloud:** [Baidu AI Cloud](/index.php?title=Baidu_AI_Cloud&action=edit&redlink=1) provides model-as-a-service, training/inference, and industry solutions, reporting 26% year-over-year revenue growth in Q4 2024 and 42% growth in Q1 2025.[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-aicloud_q4-4)[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-q1_2025_results-5)

- **Autonomous driving:** Under Apollo, Baidu operates [Apollo Go](/index.php?title=Apollo_Go&action=edit&redlink=1) robotaxis with a 1,000-vehicle global fleet. Q2 2025 saw 2.2 million fully driverless rides, with cumulative rides exceeding 14 million by August 2025.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-apollo_fleet-6)[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-apollo_rides-7)

- **Developer ecosystem:** [PaddlePaddle](/index.php?title=PaddlePaddle&action=edit&redlink=1), open-sourced in 2016, serves as China's leading deep-learning framework with over 4.77 million developers and 760,000 companies using the platform.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-paddle_ieee-8)[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-paddle_github-9)

- **AI hardware:** Through [Kunlunxin](/index.php?title=Kunlunxin&action=edit&redlink=1), Baidu designs AI accelerators, with second-generation 7nm Kunlun chips entering mass production in 2025.[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-kunlun_chip-10)

## History and Development

### Early Development (2013-2016)

Baidu began its serious investment in [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) in 2013, establishing the Institute of Deep Learning (IDL) as its first dedicated AI research laboratory.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-idl_established-11) The company was among the first Chinese technology companies to recognize the potential of [deep learning](/index.php?title=Deep_learning&action=edit&redlink=1), developing capabilities shortly after [Geoffrey Hinton](/index.php?title=Geoffrey_Hinton&action=edit&redlink=1)'s breakthrough at the ImageNet competition in 2012.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-paddle_ieee-8)

In 2014, Baidu hired [Andrew Ng](/index.php?title=Andrew_Ng&action=edit&redlink=1), co-founder of [Google Brain](/index.php?title=Google_Brain&action=edit&redlink=1) and a prominent AI researcher from [Stanford University](/index.php?title=Stanford_University&action=edit&redlink=1), as its Chief Scientist to lead Baidu Research and the company's AI initiatives.[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-andrew_ng-12) Under Ng's leadership, Baidu Research was established with labs in Silicon Valley and Beijing, and the AI group grew to approximately 1,300 people by 2017, including 300 researchers in Baidu Research.[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-andrew_ng-12)

### Formation of AI Group (2017-2018)

In January 2017, Baidu recruited former [Microsoft](/index.php?title=Microsoft&action=edit&redlink=1) executive [Qi Lu](/index.php?title=Qi_Lu&action=edit&redlink=1) as Chief Operating Officer to oversee its AI strategy. Under Lu's leadership, Baidu formally consolidated its various AI units into the **Artificial Intelligence Group (AIG)** in March 2017.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-qi_lu-13)

Following Andrew Ng's departure in March 2017, [Wang Haifeng](/index.php?title=Wang_Haifeng&action=edit&redlink=1) was appointed to lead the expanded AI division, overseeing [machine learning](/wiki/machine_learning), [big data](/index.php?title=Big_data&action=edit&redlink=1), [computer vision](/wiki/computer_vision), [natural language processing](/index.php?title=Natural_language_processing&action=edit&redlink=1), [speech technology](/index.php?title=Speech_technology&action=edit&redlink=1), [knowledge graph](/index.php?title=Knowledge_graph&action=edit&redlink=1), and [augmented reality](/wiki/augmented_reality).[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-wang_lead_aig-14)[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-wang_ieee-15)

In May 2018, following Qi Lu's departure, Wang Haifeng was promoted to Senior Vice President and General Manager of AIG.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-wang_svp-16) In May 2019, he was further promoted to Chief Technology Officer.[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-wang_cto-17)

### Recent Developments (2019-2025)

In 2018, China's government designated Baidu as one of its "AI champions," recognizing the company's leadership in artificial intelligence development.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-baidu_wikipedia-1) The company has continued to expand its AI capabilities, with the AIG growing to over 2,000 scientists and engineers.[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-aig_2000-18)

In March 2023, Baidu launched [Ernie Bot](/index.php?title=Ernie_Bot&action=edit&redlink=1) (Wenxin Yiyan), one of China's first generative AI chatbots, competing with global models like [ChatGPT](/wiki/chatgpt).[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-ernie_launch-19) The bot opened to the general public on August 31, 2023, after regulatory approval.[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-ernie_launch-19)

In March 2025, Baidu released two new AI models: [ERNIE 4.5](/index.php?title=ERNIE_4.5&action=edit&redlink=1), a [multimodal](/index.php?title=Multimodal&action=edit&redlink=1) foundation model, and [ERNIE X1](/index.php?title=ERNIE_X1&action=edit&redlink=1), a reasoning model, claiming performance on par with [DeepSeek R1](/wiki/deepseek) at half the price.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-ernie45_launch-3)

## Core Technologies

### ERNIE Foundation Models

[ERNIE](/index.php?title=ERNIE&action=edit&redlink=1) (Enhanced Representation through Knowledge Integration) is Baidu's series of [large language models](/index.php?title=Large_language_models&action=edit&redlink=1) that has been in development since 2019.[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-ernie1_release-22) The ERNIE family represents Baidu's core generative AI technology, with models evolving from knowledge-enhanced pre-training to multimodal capabilities.

#### Version History

| Version | Release Date | Parameters | Key Features |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ERNIE 1.0 | March 2019 | - | Introduced knowledge integration by masking entities and phrases, not just single words[&#91;27&#93;](#cite_note-ernie1_paper-27) |
| ERNIE 2.0 | July 2019 | - | Continual pre-training framework with multi-task learning[&#91;28&#93;](#cite_note-ernie2_paper-28) |
| ERNIE 3.0 | December 2021 | 10 billion | Unified framework excelling at both language understanding and generation[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-ernie3_release-23) |
| ERNIE 3.5 | 2023 | - | Improved training throughput 2x, inference throughput 17x vs ERNIE 3.0[&#91;29&#93;](#cite_note-ernie35_performance-29) |
| ERNIE 4.0 | October 2023 | - | Full upgrade in understanding, generation, reasoning, and memory[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-ernie4_release-25) |
| ERNIE 4.5 | March 2025 | - | Native multimodal (text, image, audio, video), priced at 1% of GPT-4.5's cost[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-ernie45_launch-3) |
| ERNIE X1 | March 2025 | - | First reasoning model with multimodal capabilities, performance on par with DeepSeek R1[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-ernie45_launch-3) |

#### ERNIE 4.5

Released in March 2025, ERNIE 4.5 is Baidu's first native [multimodal model](/wiki/multimodal_model), capable of understanding and generating text, images, audio, and video content.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-ernie45_launch-3) Key specifications:

- Input prices: RMB 0.004 per thousand tokens

- Output prices: RMB 0.016 per thousand tokens

- Performance claimed to outperform GPT-4.5 in multiple benchmarks

- Priced at approximately 1% of GPT-4.5's cost

#### ERNIE X1

ERNIE X1 is Baidu's first [reasoning model](/index.php?title=Reasoning_model&action=edit&redlink=1) with multimodal capabilities, designed for deep-thinking tasks.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-ernie45_launch-3) Features include:

- Performance comparable to [DeepSeek R1](/wiki/deepseek) at half the price

- Support for advanced search, document Q&A, image understanding, and code interpretation

- Technologies including Progressive Reinforcement Learning Method and End-to-End Training Approach

### PaddlePaddle Deep Learning Framework

[PaddlePaddle](/index.php?title=PaddlePaddle&action=edit&redlink=1) (PArallel Distributed Deep LEarning, Chinese: 飞桨; pinyin: Fēijiǎng) is Baidu's [open-source](/index.php?title=Open-source&action=edit&redlink=1) [deep learning](/index.php?title=Deep_learning&action=edit&redlink=1) platform, first developed in 2013 and open-sourced in 2016.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-paddle_ieee-8) It serves as a direct competitor to global frameworks like [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1)'s [TensorFlow](/wiki/tensorflow) and [Meta](/wiki/meta)'s [PyTorch](/index.php?title=PyTorch&action=edit&redlink=1).

Key statistics and features:

- Used by more than 4.77 million developers globally[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-paddle_ieee-8)

- Over 760,000 companies using the platform

- Generated over 1,100,000 models[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-paddle_github-9)

- Comprehensive toolsets including:
PaddleDetection (object detection)

- PaddleSeg (semantic segmentation)

- PaddleHub (pre-trained model hub)

- PaddleNLP (natural language processing)

- PaddleOCR (optical character recognition)[&#91;30&#93;](#cite_note-paddle_tools-30)

### Baidu Brain

[Baidu Brain](/index.php?title=Baidu_Brain&action=edit&redlink=1) is the company's core AI engine and technology platform, first unveiled in 2016.[&#91;20&#93;](#cite_note-brain_launch-20) It serves as the foundational infrastructure for all of Baidu's AI-related businesses and integrates:

- **[Computer vision](/wiki/computer_vision)**: Image recognition, facial recognition, and OCR

- **Natural Language Processing**: Language understanding, sentiment analysis, machine translation

- **Voice Technology**: Speech recognition and speech synthesis

- **Knowledge Graph**: Large-scale structured knowledge base

- **Deep Learning**: Underlying frameworks and models

As of 2022, Baidu Brain evolved to version 7.0, with over 1,397 technical capabilities used by 8 million developers.[&#91;31&#93;](#cite_note-brain_v7-31)
