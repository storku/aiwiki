Moonshot AI** ([Chinese](/index.php?title=Chinese_language&action=edit&redlink=1): 月之暗面; [pinyin](/index.php?title=Pinyin&action=edit&redlink=1): Yuè Zhī Ànmiàn*, literally "Dark Side of the Moon"; legal name: **Beijing Moonshot AI Technology Co., Ltd.**) is a [Chinese](/index.php?title=China&action=edit&redlink=1) [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) company headquartered in [Beijing](/index.php?title=Beijing&action=edit&redlink=1), founded in March 2023. The company specializes in developing [large language models](/index.php?title=Large_language_models&action=edit&redlink=1) (LLMs) and aims to achieve [artificial general intelligence](/wiki/artificial_general_intelligence) (AGI) through foundational models with capabilities in long context processing, multimodal world modeling, and scalable architectures for self-improvement.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-1) Moonshot AI has been dubbed one of China's "AI Tigers" or as part of the "Six Tigers" by investors, alongside companies like [Zhipu AI](/wiki/zhipu_ai), [MiniMax](/wiki/minimax), [01.AI](/index.php?title=01.AI&action=edit&redlink=1), [Baichuan AI](/index.php?title=Baichuan_AI&action=edit&redlink=1), and [DeepSeek](/wiki/deepseek).[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-quartz-2) The company is best known for its [Kimi](/wiki/kimi_chatbot) chatbot and the open-source [Kimi K2](/index.php?title=Kimi_K2&action=edit&redlink=1) model, which features advanced long-context processing capabilities.

## History

### Foundation

Moonshot AI was founded in March 2023 by [Yang Zhilin](/index.php?title=Yang_Zhilin&action=edit&redlink=1), Zhou Xinyu, and Wu Yuxin, all alumni of [Tsinghua University](/index.php?title=Tsinghua_University&action=edit&redlink=1), amid a surge in interest in generative AI following the success of [ChatGPT](/wiki/chatgpt).[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-moonshot_founding-3) The company was launched on the 50th anniversary of [Pink Floyd](/index.php?title=Pink_Floyd&action=edit&redlink=1)'s album *[The Dark Side of the Moon](/index.php?title=The_Dark_Side_of_the_Moon&action=edit&redlink=1)*, which was founder Yang Zhilin's favorite album and the inspiration for the company's Chinese name.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-1) The English name "Moonshot" reflects the ambitious and difficult nature of the company's mission, described by Yang as "like landing on the moon."[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-aiproem-4)

Yang Zhilin stated that his goal for founding Moonshot AI was to build foundational models to achieve [artificial general intelligence](/wiki/artificial_general_intelligence) (AGI), with three key milestones: long context length, multimodal world model, and a scalable general architecture capable of continuous self-improvement without human input.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-1)

### Early Development (2023-2024)

In October 2023, Moonshot launched its first product, the [Kimi](/index.php?title=Kimi&action=edit&redlink=1) chatbot, to the public. The chatbot initially could process up to 200,000 Chinese characters per conversation, making it the world's first AI assistant with such extensive context-handling capabilities.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-moonshot_founding-3)

By March 2024, Moonshot claimed Kimi could handle 2 million Chinese characters in a single prompt, a significant upgrade from the previous version.[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-reuters-kimi-2m-5) The chatbot emerged as the closest rival to [Baidu](/index.php?title=Baidu&action=edit&redlink=1)'s Ernie Bot in the Chinese market. Due to increased user demand following this upgrade, Kimi suffered a two-day outage on March 21, 2024, prompting a public apology from the company.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-1)

### Recent Developments (2025)

On January 20, 2025, Kimi K1.5 was released, which Moonshot claimed matched the performance of [OpenAI](/wiki/openai)'s o1 model in mathematics, coding, and multimodal reasoning capabilities.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-yicaiglobal-6)

In July 2025, Moonshot AI released the weights for Kimi K2, a [large language model](/wiki/large_language_model) with 1 trillion total parameters using a [mixture-of-experts](/index.php?title=Mixture-of-experts&action=edit&redlink=1) (MoE) architecture, trained on 15.5 trillion tokens and released under a modified [MIT License](/index.php?title=MIT_License&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-cnbc-k2-7)

On September 9, 2025, Moonshot AI released Kimi-K2-Instruct-0905, which increased performance in agentic coding tasks and doubled the context window to 256K tokens.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-scmp-update-8)

## Founders

### Yang Zhilin

[Yang Zhilin](/index.php?title=Yang_Zhilin&action=edit&redlink=1) (杨植麟) serves as CEO and co-founder. He holds a [Ph.D.](/index.php?title=Doctor_of_Philosophy&action=edit&redlink=1) from [Carnegie Mellon University](/index.php?title=Carnegie_Mellon_University&action=edit&redlink=1)'s Language Technologies Institute and graduated from [Tsinghua University](/index.php?title=Tsinghua_University&action=edit&redlink=1), where he ranked first in his class at the Computer Science Department.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-moonshot_founding-3) Before founding Moonshot AI, Yang worked at [Facebook AI Research](/index.php?title=Facebook_AI_Research&action=edit&redlink=1) (now [Meta AI](/wiki/meta_ai)) and [Google Brain](/index.php?title=Google_Brain&action=edit&redlink=1), where he co-authored influential papers including [Transformer-XL](/index.php?title=Transformer-XL&action=edit&redlink=1) and [XLNet](/index.php?title=XLNet&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-moonshot_founding-3) He also worked with [Huawei Technologies](/index.php?title=Huawei_Technologies&action=edit&redlink=1) on an early version of the Pangu AI model in 2020 and led a team to develop the Wudao LLM at the Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence in 2021.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-moonshot_founding-3)

### Zhou Xinyu

Zhou Xinyu (周昕宇) is a co-founder who previously worked at [Hulu](/index.php?title=Hulu&action=edit&redlink=1), [Tencent](/index.php?title=Tencent&action=edit&redlink=1), and [Megvii](/index.php?title=Megvii&action=edit&redlink=1), conducting research in deploying deep neural networks on hardware with limited computational resources.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-datainnovation-9)

### Wu Yuxin

Wu Yuxin (吴育昕) is a co-founder who previously worked at [Google Brain](/index.php?title=Google_Brain&action=edit&redlink=1) on foundation models and at [Meta AI Research](/index.php?title=Meta_AI_Research&action=edit&redlink=1) on computer vision.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-datainnovation-9)

## Products and Services

### Kimi Chatbot

[File:Kimi chatbot interface.jpg](/index.php?title=Special:Upload&wpDestFile=Kimi_chatbot_interface.jpg)  Kimi chatbot interface showing long-context capabilities

In October 2023, Moonshot launched its first [AI chatbot](/index.php?title=AI_chatbot&action=edit&redlink=1), [Kimi](/index.php?title=Kimi&action=edit&redlink=1), whose name comes from Yang's English nickname.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-1) The chatbot initially could process up to 200,000 Chinese characters per conversation, making it the world's first AI assistant with such extensive context-handling capabilities.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-moonshot_founding-3)

By March 2024, Moonshot claimed Kimi could handle 2 million Chinese characters in a single prompt, a significant upgrade from the previous version.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-1) This allows the chatbot to analyze and summarize the content of lengthy documents, such as academic papers, financial reports, or entire books, in a single query. For comparison, 2 million Chinese characters is roughly equivalent to the text of several novels.[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-reuters-kimi-2m-5)

#### Pricing

In China, Kimi offers six tiers of subscription plans ranging from 5.2 yuan for four days to 399 yuan for a year of priority access.[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-scmp-paid-10)

### Kimi K2 Model

In July 2025, Moonshot AI released the weights for [Kimi K2](/index.php?title=Kimi_K2&action=edit&redlink=1), a [large language model](/wiki/large_language_model) with 1 trillion total parameters using a [mixture-of-experts](/index.php?title=Mixture-of-experts&action=edit&redlink=1) (MoE) architecture, where 32 billion parameters are active during inference.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-arxiv_k2-11) The model was trained on 15.5 trillion tokens of data and is released under a modified [MIT License](/index.php?title=MIT_License&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-1)

Key features of Kimi K2 include:

| Feature | Specification |
| --- | --- |
| Total Parameters | 1 trillion |
| Active Parameters | 32 billion |
| Architecture | [Mixture-of-Experts](/index.php?title=Mixture-of-Experts&action=edit&redlink=1) (MoE) with 384 experts |
| Training Data | 15.5 trillion tokens |
| Context Window | 128K tokens (256K in K2-Instruct-0905) |
| License | Modified MIT License |

The model achieved state-of-the-art performance among open-source non-thinking models, with notable scores including:[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-github_k2-12)

- 65.8% on [SWE-bench](/wiki/swe-bench) Verified

- 66.1% on [Tau2-Bench](/index.php?title=Tau2-Bench&action=edit&redlink=1)

- 76.5% on [ACEBench](/index.php?title=ACEBench&action=edit&redlink=1) (English)

- 47.3% on [SWE-Bench](/index.php?title=SWE-Bench&action=edit&redlink=1) Multilingual

### Mooncake Platform

[File:Mooncake architecture.png](/index.php?title=Special:Upload&wpDestFile=Mooncake_architecture.png)  Mooncake's KVCache-centric architecture diagram

[Mooncake](/index.php?title=Mooncake&action=edit&redlink=1) is the serving platform for Moonshot's Kimi chatbot, processing over 100 billion tokens daily.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-1) It features a KVCache-centric disaggregated architecture that separates prefill and decoding clusters, utilizing underused CPU, DRAM, SSD, and NIC resources of GPU clusters.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-usenix-13)

Moonshot was awarded the Erik Riedel Best Paper Award at the USENIX FAST conference in February 2025 for the paper detailing Mooncake's architecture.[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-scmp-award-14) In practical deployments, Mooncake enabled Kimi to handle 115% and 107% more requests on NVIDIA A800 and H800 clusters, respectively, compared to previous systems.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-usenix-13)

Key components of Mooncake include:

- **Transfer Engine**: Facilitates rapid data transfer across TCP, RDMA, and NVMe over Fabric protocols

- **P2P Store**: Enables sharing of temporary objects between nodes in a cluster

- **KVCache-centric scheduler**: Maximizes throughput while meeting Service Level Objectives (SLOs)

The platform's code has been partially open-sourced on [GitHub](/index.php?title=GitHub&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-github_mooncake-15)

### Kimi Slides

In 2025, Moonshot AI launched [Kimi Slides](/index.php?title=Kimi_Slides&action=edit&redlink=1), an AI agent within the Kimi ecosystem that generates professional presentations from text prompts, documents, or website URLs.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-analyticsvidhya-16) The tool is part of Kimi+, Moonshot AI's premium version of the chatbot, though it was initially offered for free to all users.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-analyticsvidhya-16)

### OK Computer Agent Mode

In late 2025, Moonshot AI introduced "[OK Computer](/index.php?title=OK_Computer&action=edit&redlink=1)," an agent mode for Kimi that can create multi-page websites and editable slides from simple prompts. The feature supports processing up to 1 million rows of input data and multimedia outputs including text, audio, and video.[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-scmp_okcomputer-17) The name "OK Computer" is a reference to the 1997 album by [Radiohead](/index.php?title=Radiohead&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-devicology-18)

### Developer Platform

Moonshot AI provides an open platform for developers via its API service at platform.moonshot.ai. This includes access to models like Kimi K1.5, which features a 128K context window, function calling, and vision support. Pricing starts at $0.004 per 1K input tokens and $0.006 per 1K output tokens, with a free tier of 1 million tokens monthly.[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-platform-19)

## Research and Technical Contributions

### Muon Optimizer

In collaboration with [UCLA](/index.php?title=UCLA&action=edit&redlink=1), Moonshot AI researchers published "Muon is Scalable for LLM Training," demonstrating successful scaling of the [Muon](/index.php?title=Muon&action=edit&redlink=1) optimizer.[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-arxiv_muon-25) The Muon optimizer, which uses momentum orthogonalized by Newton-Schulz iterations, achieves approximately 2× computational efficiency compared to [AdamW](/index.php?title=AdamW&action=edit&redlink=1) under compute-optimal conditions.[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-marktechpost_muon-26)

Key innovations include:

- **MuonClip**: An enhanced variant that prevents training instability at massive scale

- **Weight decay integration**: Crucial for scalability to larger models

- **Per-parameter update scale adjustments**: Enhanced training stability

### Moonlight Models

Based on the Muon optimizer research, Moonshot AI released [Moonlight](/index.php?title=Moonlight&action=edit&redlink=1), a series of MoE models in 3B and 16B parameter configurations, trained with 5.7 trillion tokens.[&#91;27&#93;](#cite_note-github_moonlight-27) The models demonstrated superior performance compared to similar-scale models while requiring significantly fewer training FLOPs.[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-marktechpost_muon-26)

## Notable Events

### Exam Period Restrictions

During China's June 2025 *[gaokao](/index.php?title=Gaokao&action=edit&redlink=1)* period, Moonshot AI, along with several major tech platforms including [Baidu](/index.php?title=Baidu&action=edit&redlink=1), [ByteDance](/index.php?title=ByteDance&action=edit&redlink=1), and [Tencent](/index.php?title=Tencent&action=edit&redlink=1), temporarily restricted certain AI features to mitigate exam-related misuse and prevent cheating.[&#91;31&#93;](#cite_note-businessinsider-31)

### International Expansion Plans

In June 2024, reports suggested Moonshot AI was planning to expand into the US market with products like Ohai (a social AI app) and Noisee (an audio generation tool), but the company denied these intentions, stating no overseas products were in development.[&#91;32&#93;](#cite_note-thepaper-32)

## Controversies and Legal Issues

### Funding Controversies

Following the February 2024 funding round, reports emerged that CEO Yang Zhilin and related individuals cashed out $40 million in shares, an unusually large amount for a first-year startup, raising concerns among investors.[&#91;33&#93;](#cite_note-pandaily-33)

### Investor Disputes

In November 2024, a group of investors filed for arbitration against the company's co-founder and Chief Technology Officer, alleging that funding rounds were conducted without obtaining required consent from some AI-focused investors.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-1) The dispute involved [GSR Ventures China](/index.php?title=GSR_Ventures_China&action=edit&redlink=1) and four other firms that had invested in Yang Zhilin's previous venture, Recurrent AI.[&#91;34&#93;](#cite_note-scmp_dispute-34)

This led to reduced involvement from some investors and ongoing legal proceedings into 2025. Additional disputes involved alleged conflicts of interest related to a spin-off company and fiduciary breaches, further complicating investor relations.[&#91;35&#93;](#cite_note-ctol-35) As of February 2025, the arbitration case advanced without settlement.[&#91;36&#93;](#cite_note-technode-arbitration-36)

## Corporate Philosophy

Yang Zhilin has described his vision for Moonshot AI as combining "the technology idealism of [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) and business philosophy of [ByteDance](/index.php?title=ByteDance&action=edit&redlink=1)."[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-moonshot_founding-3) This philosophy aims to balance AGI's far-reaching potential with the need for practical, user-centered solutions that can sustain a commercially viable enterprise.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-datainnovation-9)

The company's mission is inherently global in scope. Yang has stated: "We don't want to be anything Chinese, nor necessarily OpenAI," arguing that a truly impactful AGI company cannot endure long-term if confined to a regional market.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-datainnovation-9)
