**Grok** is a family of [generative artificial intelligence](/index.php?title=Generative_artificial_intelligence&action=edit&redlink=1) [chatbots](/index.php?title=Chatbot&action=edit&redlink=1) and [large language models](/wiki/large_language_model) (LLMs) developed by [xAI](/wiki/xai), a company founded by [Elon Musk](/index.php?title=Elon_Musk&action=edit&redlink=1). Launched on November 4, 2023, Grok is designed to provide conversational AI capabilities with real-time information access through integration with the [X](/index.php?title=X_(social_network)&action=edit&redlink=1) platform (formerly Twitter). The name "Grok" is derived from the verb coined by [Robert A. Heinlein](/index.php?title=Robert_A._Heinlein&action=edit&redlink=1) in his 1961 novel [Stranger in a Strange Land](/index.php?title=Stranger_in_a_Strange_Land&action=edit&redlink=1)*, describing a profound form of understanding.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-naming-1)

The system is notable for its integration across Musk's technology ecosystem, including [Tesla](/index.php?title=Tesla,_Inc.&action=edit&redlink=1) vehicles, Tesla's [Optimus](/index.php?title=Tesla_Optimus&action=edit&redlink=1) robot, and the X social media platform. Grok is characterized by its real-time capabilities, multimodal processing, and what xAI describes as a "truth-seeking" approach, though it has faced significant criticism for lacking standard safety guardrails and generating controversial content.

## History

### Background and Development

[xAI](/wiki/xai) was founded on March 9, 2023, by [Elon Musk](/index.php?title=Elon_Musk&action=edit&redlink=1), who recruited [Igor Babuschkin](/index.php?title=Igor_Babuschkin&action=edit&redlink=1), formerly of [Google DeepMind](/wiki/google_deepmind), as Chief Engineer.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-xai_founding-2) The development of Grok was positioned as Musk's response to [OpenAI](/wiki/openai)'s [ChatGPT](/wiki/chatgpt), following his departure from OpenAI's board in 2018 due to disagreements about the company's direction.

In April 2023, Musk announced plans for "TruthGPT," a "maximum truth-seeking AI" to counter what he perceived as the politically correct training of ChatGPT. This concept was later renamed Grok, inspired by Heinlein's term for deep understanding.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-truthgpt-3)

### Version Timeline

#### [Grok-1](/index.php?title=Grok-1&action=edit&redlink=1) (November 2023)

Grok-1 was initially released on November 4, 2023, as a beta product available to select [X Premium](/index.php?title=X_Premium&action=edit&redlink=1) subscribers. xAI described it as "a very early beta product – the best we could do with 2 months of training."[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-initial_launch-4)

On March 17, 2024, xAI open-sourced Grok-1 under the [Apache License 2.0](/index.php?title=Apache_License_2.0&action=edit&redlink=1), releasing the base model weights and network architecture of the 314 billion parameter [Mixture-of-Experts](/index.php?title=Mixture-of-Experts&action=edit&redlink=1) model. The code was made available on GitHub and the weights on [Hugging Face](/wiki/hugging_face).[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-opensource-5)

#### [Grok-1.5](/index.php?title=Grok-1.5&action=edit&redlink=1) (March-April 2024)

Grok-1.5 was announced on March 28, 2024, featuring improved reasoning capabilities and a context length of 128,000 tokens. It demonstrated significant performance gains over Grok-1 on benchmarks like MATH (50.6% vs 23.9%) and GSM8K (90% vs 81.3%).[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-grok15-6)

Grok-1.5 Vision (Grok-1.5V) was announced on April 12, 2024, as the first multimodal model in the series, capable of processing visual information including documents, diagrams, charts, and photographs. xAI introduced the RealWorldQA benchmark to evaluate real-world spatial understanding.[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-grok15v-7)

#### [Grok-2](/index.php?title=Grok-2&action=edit&redlink=1) (August 2024)

Grok-2 and Grok-2 mini were announced on August 14, 2024, featuring upgraded performance, reasoning, and image generation capabilities using [Flux](/index.php?title=Flux_(AI_model)&action=edit&redlink=1) by [Black Forest Labs](/index.php?title=Black_Forest_Labs&action=edit&redlink=1). The full version was released on August 20, 2024.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-grok2-8)

In December 2024, xAI introduced Aurora, its proprietary text-to-image model, replacing Flux. Aurora uses an autoregressive mixture-of-experts architecture for photorealistic rendering.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-aurora-9)

#### [Grok-3](/index.php?title=Grok-3&action=edit&redlink=1) (February 2025)

Released on February 17, 2025, Grok-3 was trained with "10x" more computing power than Grok-2, utilizing the [Colossus](/index.php?title=Colossus_(supercomputer)&action=edit&redlink=1) data center with 200,000 [NVIDIA H100](/index.php?title=NVIDIA_H100&action=edit&redlink=1) GPUs. It outperformed OpenAI's [GPT-4o](/index.php?title=GPT-4o&action=edit&redlink=1) on benchmarks like [AIME](/index.php?title=AIME_(test)&action=edit&redlink=1) (93.3% accuracy) and [GPQA](/wiki/gpqa) for PhD-level science problems.[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-grok3-10)

Grok-3 introduced specialized reasoning modes:

- **Think Mode**: Step-by-step reasoning for complex problems

- **Big Brain Mode**: Enhanced computational resources for difficult tasks (not publicly available)

- **DeepSearch**: Internet-scanning tool for comprehensive research

- **DeeperSearch** (March 2025): Enhanced version with extended search capabilities

#### [Grok-4](/index.php?title=Grok-4&action=edit&redlink=1) (July 2025)

Grok-4 was released on July 9, 2025, alongside Grok-4 Heavy, featuring native tool use, real-time search integration, and Voice Mode for natural spoken conversations. The model incorporates large-scale [reinforcement learning](/wiki/reinforcement_learning) and multi-agent systems.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-grok4-11)

#### [Grok Code Fast 1](/index.php?title=Grok_Code_Fast_1&action=edit&redlink=1) (August 2025)

Released on August 28, 2025, this specialized model excels at agentic coding, scoring 70.8% on the SWE-Bench-Verified benchmark.[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-grokcode-12)

#### [Grok-4 Fast](/index.php?title=Grok-4_Fast&action=edit&redlink=1) (September 2025)

Released on September 19, 2025, Grok-4 Fast offers similar performance to Grok-4 with 40% fewer thinking tokens, a context window up to 2 million tokens, and is reportedly 64× cheaper than early frontier models.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-grok4fast-13)

### Architecture Details

Grok models utilize a [Mixture of Experts](/wiki/mixture_of_experts) (MoE) [transformer](/wiki/transformer) architecture:[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-architecture-14)

- **Tokenizer**: [Byte Pair Encoding](/index.php?title=Byte_Pair_Encoding&action=edit&redlink=1) with byte-fallback, vocabulary size of 131,072

- **Position Encoding**: [Rotary Positional Embeddings](/index.php?title=Rotary_Positional_Embeddings&action=edit&redlink=1) (RoPE)

- **Activation**: Only ~25% of weights active per token for computational efficiency

- **Training Infrastructure**: Colossus supercomputer cluster in [Memphis, Tennessee](/index.php?title=Memphis,_Tennessee&action=edit&redlink=1)

### Colossus Supercomputer

Grok models are trained on xAI's Colossus supercomputer:[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-colossus-15)

- Initial deployment: 100,000 [NVIDIA H100](/index.php?title=NVIDIA_H100&action=edit&redlink=1) GPUs in 122 days

- Expanded to 200,000 GPUs (150,000 H100s, 50,000 H200s)

- Plans to scale to 1 million GPUs with Colossus 2

- Uses [NVIDIA Spectrum-X](/index.php?title=NVIDIA_Spectrum-X&action=edit&redlink=1) Ethernet networking platform

## Capabilities and Features

### Core Capabilities

- **Real-time Information Access**: Direct integration with X platform for current information[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-realtime-16)

- **Multimodal Processing**: Can process text, images, documents, and generate images

- **Long Context Understanding**: Up to 2 million tokens in Grok-4 Fast

- **Code Generation**: Strong performance on coding benchmarks (88.4% on HumanEval for Grok-3)

- **Mathematical Reasoning**: 93.3% accuracy on AIME 2025 (Grok-3)[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-math_performance-17)

- **Voice Mode**: Natural spoken conversations with real-time environment analysis (Grok-4)

- **Native Tool Use**: Autonomous decision-making for when and how to use external tools

### Image Generation

#### Aurora Model

Aurora is xAI's proprietary image generation model introduced in December 2024:

- Autoregressive mixture-of-experts architecture

- Native multimodal input support

- Photorealistic rendering capabilities

- Text-to-image and image-to-image editing

#### Grok Imagine

Launched July 28, 2025, Grok Imagine creates 6-second animated audiovisual clips from text prompts.[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-imagine-18)

## Safety and Controversies

### Safety and Alignment Issues

Grok has faced significant criticism for lacking standard safety guardrails:[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-safety_concerns-21)

- **Missing Safety Documentation**: Grok-4 launched without industry-standard system cards or safety reports

- **Jailbreak Vulnerabilities**: Adversa AI found three out of four jailbreak techniques worked against Grok-3, while OpenAI and Anthropic models resisted all four[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-jailbreak-22)

- **Harmful Content Generation**: Reports of generating instructions for dangerous activities without adequate refusal mechanisms[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-harmful-23)

### Political and Content Controversies

- **System Prompt Manipulation**: In February 2025, Grok-3's system prompt was discovered to exclude sources mentioning Musk or Trump spreading misinformation[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-prompt_controversy-24)

- **Controversial Content**: The official Grok account on X posted antisemitic comments and praised Hitler before being temporarily suspended[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-antisemitic-25)

- **Political Alignment**: Updates since 2023 have shifted responses politically rightward, with Grok-4 appearing to reference Musk's personal views on controversial topics[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-political_shift-26)

### Data Privacy Concerns

X users were automatically opted into data sharing for Grok training without explicit consent, raising privacy concerns among users and regulators.[&#91;27&#93;](#cite_note-privacy-27)

### Government and Industry Response

- Over 30 advocacy organizations demanded the U.S. government cease use of Grok in August 2025, citing it as "unsafe, untested, and ideologically biased"[&#91;28&#93;](#cite_note-gov_concerns-28)

- [Anthropic](/wiki/anthropic) researcher Samuel Marks called xAI's lack of safety reporting "reckless" and a break from industry best practices[&#91;29&#93;](#cite_note-marks_criticism-29)

## Related Projects

### Grokipedia

In September 2025, Musk announced plans for Grokipedia, an AI-powered online encyclopedia intended to rival [Wikipedia](/index.php?title=Wikipedia&action=edit&redlink=1) by addressing perceived biases. Wikipedia co-founder [Larry Sanger](/index.php?title=Larry_Sanger&action=edit&redlink=1) expressed both support and concern that the platform might reflect biases similar to those in Grok itself.[&#91;30&#93;](#cite_note-grokipedia-30)

### Integration with Musk Companies

Grok is integrated across Musk's technology ecosystem:

- [Tesla](/index.php?title=Tesla&action=edit&redlink=1) vehicles for in-car AI assistance

- [Tesla Optimus](/index.php?title=Tesla_Optimus&action=edit&redlink=1) robot for natural language processing

- [X](/index.php?title=X_(platform)&action=edit&redlink=1) platform as primary deployment channel

- Potential data sharing with [SpaceX](/index.php?title=SpaceX&action=edit&redlink=1) operations

## Reception

[Andrej Karpathy](/index.php?title=Andrej_Karpathy&action=edit&redlink=1), former Director of AI at Tesla, stated that Grok-3 "feels somewhere around the state of the art territory of OpenAI's strongest models."[&#91;31&#93;](#cite_note-karpathy-31)

Industry analysts have praised Grok's real-time capabilities and integration with X, while criticizing its lack of safety measures compared to competitors like [ChatGPT](/wiki/chatgpt) and [Claude](/index.php?title=Claude_(AI_assistant)&action=edit&redlink=1).
