**Cohere Inc.** is a Canadian [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) company that develops [large language models](/wiki/large_language_model) (LLMs) and AI-powered enterprise solutions. Founded in 2019 in Toronto by Aidan Gomez, Ivan Zhang, and Nick Frosst, the company has positioned itself as a security-first, cloud-agnostic AI provider focused exclusively on enterprise applications rather than consumer products.[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-enterprise-focus-5) Unlike competitors such as [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) (which gained prominence through consumer-facing [ChatGPT](/wiki/chatgpt)) and [Anthropic](/wiki/anthropic) (which offers both consumer and enterprise AI through [Claude](/wiki/claude)), Cohere has deliberately concentrated on building foundation models and tools optimized for retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), enterprise search, and multilingual applications.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-sacra-comparison-6)

As of September 2025, Cohere has raised approximately $1.6 billion in funding and is valued at roughly $7 billion.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-valuation-7b-2) The company reached approximately $240 million in annual recurring revenue (ARR) in 2025, surpassing its internal $200 million target.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-revenue-2025-1)

## History

### Founding and Early Development (2019-2021)

Cohere was founded in September 2019 by three individuals with deep roots in the AI research community. Aidan Gomez, the company's CEO, is one of the eight co-authors of the landmark 2017 paper "[Attention Is All You Need](/wiki/attention_is_all_you_need)," which introduced the [transformer](/wiki/transformer_(machine_learning_model)) architecture that underpins virtually all modern large language models, including [GPT-4](/wiki/gpt-4), [Claude](/wiki/claude), and [Gemini](/index.php?title=Gemini_(language_model)&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-gomez-transformer-7) Gomez contributed to the paper as a 20-year-old intern at [Google Brain](/index.php?title=Google_Brain&action=edit&redlink=1) while still completing his undergraduate degree at the University of Toronto.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-gomez-age-8)

Nick Frosst, another co-founder, was one of [Geoffrey Hinton](/index.php?title=Geoffrey_Hinton&action=edit&redlink=1)'s earliest hires at Google Brain's Toronto lab, where he worked from 2016 to 2020 on deep learning research, including capsule networks, adversarial examples, and explainability.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-frosst-bio-9) Frosst earned his computer science degree from the University of Toronto. Outside of AI, he is also known as the lead singer of the indie rock band Good Kid.[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-frosst-wikipedia-10)

Ivan Zhang, the third co-founder, studied computer science at the University of Toronto before dropping out to pursue software engineering roles. He had previously founded for.ai (later rebranded as Cohere Labs), the research lab that would become Cohere's open science arm. Zhang served as Cohere's CTO, leading the company's product organization and developer experience.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-zhang-bio-11)

All three founders attended the University of Toronto, a city and institution with deep historical ties to modern AI research through the work of Geoffrey Hinton, who pioneered [deep learning](/wiki/deep_learning) techniques at the university over several decades.[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-uoft-connections-12)

### Series A and Growth (2021-2022)

In September 2021, Cohere raised $40 million in Series A funding led by Index Ventures, with participation from Radical Ventures, Section 32, and notable AI researchers including Geoffrey Hinton, [Fei-Fei Li](/index.php?title=Fei-Fei_Li&action=edit&redlink=1), Pieter Abbeel, and Raquel Urtasun. Index Ventures partner Mike Volpi joined Cohere's board of directors.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-series-a-13)

In February 2022, the company announced $125 million in Series B funding led by Tiger Global, signaling growing investor confidence in enterprise-focused AI companies.[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-series-b-14)

### Unicorn Status and Series C (2023)

In June 2023, Cohere raised $270 million in Series C funding at a valuation of $2.2 billion, achieving unicorn status approximately four years after its founding. The round was led by Inovia Capital and included major strategic investors such as [NVIDIA](/index.php?title=NVIDIA&action=edit&redlink=1), [Oracle](/index.php?title=Oracle_Corporation&action=edit&redlink=1), and [Salesforce](/index.php?title=Salesforce&action=edit&redlink=1) Ventures, alongside financial investors including Mirae Asset, Schroders Capital, Thomvest Ventures, and Index Ventures.[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-series-c-15)

### Series D and Rapid Scaling (2024)

In July 2024, Cohere closed a $500 million Series D round at a $5.5 billion valuation, more than doubling its valuation from just one year earlier. The initial $450 million tranche came from returning investors including NVIDIA and Salesforce Ventures, with new investors such as [Cisco](/index.php?title=Cisco&action=edit&redlink=1), Canada's PSP Investments (a major pension fund), AMD Ventures, Export Development Canada (EDC), and Fujitsu. This brought Cohere's total funding to approximately $970 million.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-series-d-16)

In December 2024, the Canadian federal government announced an investment of up to $240 million in Cohere as the first funding recipient under Canada's new $2 billion Canadian Sovereign AI Compute Strategy. The investment supported Cohere's $725 million project to build a domestic AI data center in Cambridge, Ontario, in partnership with CoreWeave.[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-canada-sovereign-17)

### 2025 Developments

In January 2025, Cohere introduced North, its enterprise AI agent platform, initially launching North for Banking in partnership with RBC (Royal Bank of Canada).[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-north-launch-18)

In March 2025, Cohere released Command A, its most performant model at the time, delivering 150% higher throughput than its predecessor Command R+ while requiring only two GPUs to run.[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-command-a-19)

In August 2025, Cohere raised an additional $500 million at a $6.8 billion valuation in a round led by Radical Ventures and Inovia Capital. Alongside the funding, the company announced the hiring of Joelle Pineau as its first Chief AI Officer. Pineau, a McGill University professor and Canadian AI scientist, had previously served as Meta's VP of AI Research, overseeing the Fundamental AI Research (FAIR) lab for nearly eight years.[&#91;20&#93;](#cite_note-2025-funding-pineau-20)

In September 2025, Cohere announced a second close of this funding round, raising an additional $100 million and pushing its valuation to approximately $7 billion. Simultaneously, the company announced an expanded partnership with AMD to optimize its models for AMD Instinct MI300X accelerators, offering enterprise customers an alternative to [NVIDIA](/index.php?title=NVIDIA&action=edit&redlink=1)-only infrastructure.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-valuation-7b-2)[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-amd-partnership-21)

## Products and Technology

Cohere offers a suite of interconnected AI products designed for enterprise use cases, with a particular emphasis on retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), search, and multilingual processing.

### Command Models

The Command family is Cohere's flagship line of text generation models, optimized for enterprise tasks including tool use, RAG, agentic workflows, and multilingual applications.

| Model | Parameters | Context Length | Release Date | Key Features |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Command R | 32 billion | 128K tokens | March 2024 | Optimized for RAG and tool use; open weights (CC-BY-NC) |
| Command R+ | 104 billion | 128K tokens | April 2024 | Advanced reasoning and language understanding; open weights |
| Command R (08-2024) | 32 billion | 128K tokens | August 2024 | 50% higher throughput, 20% lower latency vs. predecessor |
| Command R+ (08-2024) | 104 billion | 128K tokens | August 2024 | Improved tool use, instruction following |
| Command A (03-2025) | 111 billion | 256K tokens | March 2025 | 150% throughput improvement; runs on two GPUs (A100/H100) |
| Command A Vision (07-2025) | 112 billion | 128K tokens | July 2025 | First commercial multimodal model; supports up to 20 images; OCR, chart interpretation |

Command A, released in March 2025, is Cohere's most performant text model, excelling at real-world enterprise tasks. With 111 billion parameters and a 256K context length, it offers a considerable increase in inference-time efficiency, requiring only two GPUs (A100s or H100s) to run.[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-command-a-19)

Command A Vision, released in July 2025, extended the Command A architecture with multimodal capabilities, enabling document analysis, chart interpretation, optical character recognition (OCR), and multilingual image processing. Benchmarks showed an average score of 83.1% compared to GPT 4.1's 78.6%, Llama 4 Maverick's 80.5%, and Mistral Medium 3's 78.3%.[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-command-a-vision-22)

Cohere has released open weights for several Command models under the CC-BY-NC (Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial) license, making them available on platforms such as [Hugging Face](/wiki/hugging_face) for research purposes.[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-open-weights-23)

### Embed

Embed is Cohere's family of embedding models that transform text and images into numerical vector representations (embeddings), enabling semantic search, retrieval, and classification in enterprise applications.

The latest version, Embed 4 (released in 2025), is a multimodal embedding model that processes both text and images, including interleaved content. It natively handles documents with tables, graphs, diagrams, code snippets, and handwritten notes. Key features include support for over 100 languages, configurable Matryoshka Embeddings in dimensions of 256, 512, 1024, and 1536, a context length of 128K tokens, and compressed embeddings that reduce vector storage costs by up to 83%.[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-embed-4-24)

Embed 4 is available on the Cohere Platform, [Amazon SageMaker](/index.php?title=Amazon_SageMaker&action=edit&redlink=1), [Amazon Bedrock](/index.php?title=Amazon_Bedrock&action=edit&redlink=1), and Azure AI Foundry.[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-embed-4-24)

### Rerank

Rerank is a search foundation model that reorders search results to surface the most relevant documents for a given query. In enterprise RAG pipelines, the Rerank step can significantly reduce [hallucination](/index.php?title=Hallucination_(artificial_intelligence)&action=edit&redlink=1) by ensuring the language model receives the most contextually appropriate documents.

Rerank 3.5, the latest version as of 2025, supports over 100 languages and processes a wide range of data formats, including lengthy documents, emails, tables, JSON, and code. According to Cohere, it improves enterprise search accuracy by 30% and cross-language search accuracy by 26.4% over its predecessor.[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-rerank-35-25)

The model can be integrated into existing search systems through a single API call, whether keyword-based or semantic, and can be privately deployed in virtual private cloud (VPC) or on-premises environments for full data privacy control.[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-rerank-35-25)

### North

North is Cohere's enterprise AI agent platform, introduced in January 2025. It combines large language models, search capabilities, and automation tools in a secure package that allows organizations to deploy AI while maintaining full control over sensitive data.[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-north-launch-18)

Key features of North include:

- **Chat and search**: Users can get answers to queries, summarize meeting transcripts, write marketing copy, and access information from both internal resources and the web
- **Low-code agent building**: Enterprises can build and deploy AI agents across different business functions without extensive development resources
- **Private deployment**: The platform operates in private cloud environments or on-premises installations, targeting regulated industries like finance and healthcare
- **Security and compliance**: Granular access control, agent autonomy policies, continuous red-teaming, third-party security testing, and compliance with international standards including GDPR, SOC-2, and ISO 27001

North is built on Compass, Cohere's proprietary multimodal AI search and discovery framework. Early customers and pilots include RBC, Dell, LG, Ensemble Health Partners, and Palantir. AMD has also integrated North into its internal enterprise AI portfolio.[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-north-details-26)[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-amd-partnership-21)

### Compass

Compass is Cohere's multimodal, multilingual, and format-agnostic search and discovery platform for enterprise environments. It is designed to unlock insights from fragmented data sources and integrates retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) applications with internal knowledge stores.[&#91;27&#93;](#cite_note-compass-27)

Compass is built on Cohere's Embed and Rerank models and offers:

- Pre-processing of documents in formats like PDFs, PPTs, DOCX, and XLSX
- Understanding of images, slides, and spreadsheets
- Role-based access controls and document-level security
- Deployment into any VPC or on-premises environment
- Automatic index management

### Coral

Coral was Cohere's earlier enterprise assistant product (preceding North), designed as the primary orchestration interface and knowledge assistant for enterprise workflows. Coral served as the user-facing layer that mediated interactions between users and the underlying RAG pipeline built on Cohere's Embed, Rerank, and Command models. Many of Coral's capabilities have since been incorporated into the North platform.[&#91;28&#93;](#cite_note-coral-28)

### Cohere Toolkit

Cohere Toolkit is an open-source collection of prebuilt components that enables developers to quickly build and deploy RAG applications. Available on GitHub, the toolkit provides building blocks for developing scalable AI applications like knowledge assistants tailored to enterprise data. It supports deployment on [AWS](/index.php?title=Amazon_Web_Services&action=edit&redlink=1), [Google Cloud](/index.php?title=Google_Cloud&action=edit&redlink=1), and [Azure](/index.php?title=Microsoft_Azure&action=edit&redlink=1), and includes 100+ pre-built connectors for enterprise data sources.[&#91;29&#93;](#cite_note-toolkit-29)

## Enterprise RAG Architecture

A central part of Cohere's value proposition is its end-to-end RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) stack, which combines its individual products into an integrated pipeline for enterprise knowledge retrieval and generation.

The typical Cohere RAG workflow involves three stages:

1. **Embedding** (via Embed): Enterprise documents are converted into vector representations and stored in a vector database
2. **Retrieval and re-ranking** (via Rerank): When a user submits a query, relevant documents are retrieved from the vector store and then re-ranked by semantic relevance using Rerank
3. **Generation** (via Command): The top-ranked documents are passed as context to a Command model, which generates a grounded response with citations

This architecture is designed to reduce hallucinations by ensuring that the language model's output is anchored in actual enterprise documents rather than relying solely on the model's parametric knowledge.[&#91;30&#93;](#cite_note-rag-architecture-30)

## Multilingual Capabilities and Aya Models

### Cohere Labs and Open Science

Cohere Labs (formerly Cohere For AI) is the company's dedicated research arm, focused on advancing AI through transparent research, open-weights models, and a global research community. The lab's Open Science Community includes over 4,500 members from 150 countries. In its first year of operation, Cohere Labs launched 15 open science initiatives and supported the publication of more than 25 research papers.[&#91;31&#93;](#cite_note-cohere-labs-31)

Programs run by Cohere Labs include Catalyst Grants (providing free API access to academic partners, civic institutions, and impact-focused organizations) and the Scholars Program (offering mentorship for emerging researchers with access to research infrastructure).[&#91;31&#93;](#cite_note-cohere-labs-31)

### Aya Model Family

The Aya project is Cohere Labs' flagship multilingual initiative and one of the largest open science collaborations in machine learning, involving over 3,000 researchers worldwide. The Aya models represent Cohere's commitment to expanding AI capabilities beyond English and high-resource languages.

| Model | Parameters | Languages | Release | License | Key Features |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Aya 101 | N/A | 101 languages | 2024 | Open weights | First model covering 50+ underserved languages; instruction-tuned |
| Aya 23 (8B) | 8 billion | 23 languages | May 2024 | Open weights | Lightweight multilingual model |
| Aya 23 (35B) | 35 billion | 23 languages | May 2024 | Open weights | High-performance multilingual model |
| Aya Expanse | Various | 101 languages | 2024 | Open weights | Cross-lingual transfer; 30% reduced infrastructure costs |
| Aya Vision (8B) | 8 billion | 23 languages | March 2025 | CC-BY-NC | Multimodal; outperforms Qwen2.5-VL 7B, Pixtral 12B |
| Aya Vision (32B) | 32 billion | 23 languages | March 2025 | CC-BY-NC | Multimodal vision and language understanding |

**Aya 101** covers over 101 languages, including more than 50 that had been historically underserved by AI systems. The model was developed through a global collaborative effort and established a new standard for inclusive AI by achieving state-of-the-art performance across diverse benchmarks while maintaining efficiency for both low-resource and high-resource languages.[&#91;32&#93;](#cite_note-aya-101-32)

**Aya 23** was released in May 2024 as a family of open-weights models in 8B and 35B parameter sizes, optimized for 23 languages: Arabic, Chinese (simplified and traditional), Czech, Dutch, English, French, German, Greek, Hebrew, Hindi, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Persian, Polish, Portuguese, Romanian, Russian, Spanish, Turkish, Ukrainian, and Vietnamese.[&#91;33&#93;](#cite_note-aya-23-33)

**Aya Vision**, released in March 2025, added multimodal capabilities to the Aya family, combining a language model with a SigLIP vision encoder to enable tasks like image captioning, visual question answering, and multilingual document understanding. The 8B variant outperformed leading models such as Qwen2.5-VL 7B, Pixtral 12B, Gemini Flash 1.5 8B, and Llama-3.2 11B Vision by up to 79% win rates on the AyaVisionBench benchmark. The open weights for both variants are available on Hugging Face and Kaggle.[&#91;34&#93;](#cite_note-aya-vision-34)

## Enterprise Strategy and Differentiators

### Cloud-Agnostic Deployment

One of Cohere's most significant differentiators is its cloud-agnostic deployment strategy. Unlike [OpenAI](/wiki/openai), which is closely aligned with [Microsoft](/index.php?title=Microsoft&action=edit&redlink=1) Azure, and [Anthropic](/wiki/anthropic), which has a deep partnership with [Amazon Web Services](/index.php?title=Amazon_Web_Services&action=edit&redlink=1), Cohere's models can run on AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI), or in customers' own data centers.[&#91;35&#93;](#cite_note-cloud-agnostic-35)

This approach avoids hyperscaler lock-in, which is a significant selling point for enterprises that want assurance their AI provider is not dependent on a single cloud vendor. Cohere offers multiple deployment options:

| Deployment Model | Description | Use Case |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cohere Platform | Managed API access | Quick prototyping and development |
| Amazon Bedrock / SageMaker | Managed access on AWS | AWS-native enterprises |
| Azure AI Foundry | Managed access on Azure | Microsoft-ecosystem enterprises |
| Google Cloud | Managed access on GCP | Google Cloud customers |
| Oracle Cloud (OCI) | Managed access on Oracle | Oracle enterprise customers |
| Bring Your Own Cloud (BYOC) | Model deployed in customer's VPC | Data residency requirements |
| On-premises | Fully private deployment | Maximum security; regulated industries |

### Data Privacy and Security

Approximately 85% of Cohere's revenue comes from private deployments, primarily serving finance, healthcare, and government sectors where data privacy is paramount.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-sacra-comparison-6) The company's "Bring Your Own Cloud" model keeps model weights and telemetry within the customer's virtual private cloud, ensuring no data leaves the customer's environment.

For organizations handling data that cannot reside on any cloud, Cohere also supports fully private on-premises deployment. This approach contrasts with many competitors whose models are primarily available through public cloud APIs.[&#91;35&#93;](#cite_note-cloud-agnostic-35)

### Enterprise-Only Focus

Cohere has deliberately chosen not to build consumer-facing products. The company has no equivalent to ChatGPT or Claude.ai for general consumers. Instead, every product is designed for B2B enterprise use cases, including customer support automation, internal knowledge management, document processing, and workflow automation. This singular focus allows Cohere to optimize its models, infrastructure, and go-to-market strategy entirely around enterprise requirements such as compliance, integration, customization, and scalability.[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-enterprise-focus-5)

## Key Partnerships

### Oracle

Cohere has a deep strategic partnership with Oracle. Cohere uses Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI) as a primary platform for training and deploying its models, and Oracle has integrated Cohere's models into its Oracle Cloud Generative AI services. Cohere's language models are embedded in Oracle's business applications, including Oracle Fusion Cloud, Oracle NetSuite, and Oracle's industry-specific applications, powering over 100 generative AI use cases across finance and supply chain management.[&#91;36&#93;](#cite_note-oracle-partnership-36)

### AMD

In September 2025, AMD and Cohere announced an expanded global collaboration to optimize Cohere's enterprise LLMs on AMD Instinct GPU infrastructure. Cohere's models (including Command A, Command A Vision, and Command A Translate) are available on AMD Instinct MI300X accelerators, providing enterprise customers with an alternative to NVIDIA-dominated infrastructure. AMD also integrated Cohere's North platform into its internal enterprise AI workloads.[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-amd-partnership-21)
