Meta AI** is a [research division](/index.php?title=Research_division&action=edit&redlink=1) of [Meta](/index.php?title=Meta_Platforms&action=edit&redlink=1) (formerly Facebook) that develops [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence), [augmented reality](/wiki/augmented_reality), and [superintelligence](/index.php?title=Superintelligence&action=edit&redlink=1) technologies. The division encompasses both Meta's AI research efforts through [Meta Superintelligence Labs](/index.php?title=Meta_Superintelligence_Labs&action=edit&redlink=1) (MSL) and an AI assistant product available across Meta's platforms.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-meta-ai-wiki-1)

## History

### Foundation and Early Years (2013-2016)

Meta AI was originally founded on December 11, 2013 under the name **Facebook Artificial Intelligence Research (FAIR)**.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-founding-2) The foundation of the laboratory was announced by CEO [Mark Zuckerberg](/index.php?title=Mark_Zuckerberg&action=edit&redlink=1) with the goal of advancing the field of [machine learning](/wiki/machine_learning) and artificial intelligence through open research. FAIR was first directed by [New York University](/index.php?title=New_York_University&action=edit&redlink=1)'s [Yann LeCun](/index.php?title=Yann_LeCun&action=edit&redlink=1), a [deep learning](/index.php?title=Deep_learning&action=edit&redlink=1) professor and [Turing Award](/index.php?title=Turing_Award&action=edit&redlink=1) winner, alongside co-founder [Rob Fergus](/index.php?title=Rob_Fergus&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-meta-ai-wiki-1)

The initial goal of FAIR, working with [NYU's Center for Data Science](/index.php?title=NYU%27s_Center_for_Data_Science&action=edit&redlink=1), was to research [data science](/index.php?title=Data_science&action=edit&redlink=1), machine learning, and artificial intelligence.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-3) Initial hubs opened in Menlo Park and New York City, specifically at [Astor Place](/index.php?title=Astor_Place&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-founding-2) [Vladimir Vapnik](/index.php?title=Vladimir_Vapnik&action=edit&redlink=1), a pioneer in [statistical learning](/index.php?title=Statistical_learning&action=edit&redlink=1), joined FAIR in 2014.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-3)

### Expansion and Growth (2015-2020)

FAIR expanded its presence globally by opening research centers in multiple locations:

[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-fair5-4)

The Paris lab, opened in June 2015, marked the first major U.S. tech AI center in continental Europe.[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-paris-5) FAIR Montreal began in September 2017 under [Joelle Pineau](/index.php?title=Joelle_Pineau&action=edit&redlink=1), expanding Canadian federal AI initiatives.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-montreal-6)

In 2016, FAIR partnered with [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1), [Amazon](/index.php?title=Amazon&action=edit&redlink=1), [IBM](/index.php?title=IBM&action=edit&redlink=1), and [Microsoft](/index.php?title=Microsoft&action=edit&redlink=1) in creating the [Partnership on Artificial Intelligence](/index.php?title=Partnership_on_Artificial_Intelligence&action=edit&redlink=1) to Benefit People and Society.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-wikipedia-3) By 2018, FAIR had approximately 200 staff members, with [Jérôme Pesenti](/index.php?title=J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_Pesenti&action=edit&redlink=1) as president and [Yann LeCun](/index.php?title=Yann_LeCun&action=edit&redlink=1) as chief AI scientist.[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-fair5-4)

### Rebranding to Meta AI (2021-Present)

Following the rebranding of Facebook, Inc. to Meta Platforms Inc. in October 2021, FAIR was renamed to Meta AI.[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-rebrand-7) This change reflected the company's broader focus on the [metaverse](/index.php?title=Metaverse&action=edit&redlink=1) and advanced AI technologies.

In January 2022, Meta unveiled the [Research SuperCluster](/index.php?title=Research_SuperCluster&action=edit&redlink=1) (RSC), a 5-exaflop AI [supercomputer](/index.php?title=Supercomputer&action=edit&redlink=1) built with 16,000 [NVIDIA](/wiki/nvidia) A100 [GPUs](/index.php?title=GPU&action=edit&redlink=1), accelerating model training up to 20× versus previous clusters.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-rsc-8)

### Formation of Meta Superintelligence Labs (2025)

In June 2025, following concerns about the company's AI competitiveness, [Mark Zuckerberg](/index.php?title=Mark_Zuckerberg&action=edit&redlink=1) announced the formation of [Meta Superintelligence Labs](/index.php?title=Meta_Superintelligence_Labs&action=edit&redlink=1) (MSL), a new division focused on achieving [artificial general intelligence](/wiki/artificial_general_intelligence) and superintelligence.[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-msl-announcement-9)

The formation came after Meta's aggressive acquisition and hiring strategy, including:

- A $14.3 billion investment in [Scale AI](/index.php?title=Scale_AI&action=edit&redlink=1) for a 49% non-voting stake, with founder [Alexandr Wang](/index.php?title=Alexandr_Wang&action=edit&redlink=1) joining as Chief AI Officer[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-scale-investment-10)

- Failed attempts to acquire [Safe Superintelligence](/index.php?title=Safe_Superintelligence&action=edit&redlink=1) (valued at $32 billion) and [Perplexity AI](/index.php?title=Perplexity_AI&action=edit&redlink=1)[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-ssi-attempt-11)

- Recruitment of [Daniel Gross](/index.php?title=Daniel_Gross&action=edit&redlink=1) (CEO of Safe Superintelligence) and [Nat Friedman](/index.php?title=Nat_Friedman&action=edit&redlink=1) (former [GitHub](/index.php?title=GitHub&action=edit&redlink=1) CEO)[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-ssi-attempt-11)

MSL comprises four groups:

- TBD Lab - managing Meta's [large language models](/index.php?title=Large_language_models&action=edit&redlink=1), led by Wang

- FAIR - artificial intelligence research team

- Products and Applied Research - consumer integration team led by Friedman

- MSL Infra - infrastructure team led by Aparna Ramani[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-msl-wiki-12)

## Research Areas and Contributions

### PyTorch Development

One of Meta AI's most significant contributions to the AI community is [PyTorch](/index.php?title=PyTorch&action=edit&redlink=1), a machine learning library released in 2017.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-pytorch-wikipedia-13) PyTorch was developed as a successor to the [Torch](/index.php?title=Torch&action=edit&redlink=1) library and quickly gained traction among researchers for its developer-friendly approach and flexibility.[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-pytorch-hist-14)

Key milestones in PyTorch's development:

- **January 2017**: Initial public release

- **March 2018**: Merger with [Caffe2](/index.php?title=Caffe2&action=edit&redlink=1)

- **December 2018**: PyTorch 1.0 release

- **September 2022**: [PyTorch Foundation](/index.php?title=PyTorch_Foundation&action=edit&redlink=1) established under the [Linux Foundation](/index.php?title=Linux_Foundation&action=edit&redlink=1)[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-pytorch-foundation-15)

- **March 2023**: PyTorch 2.0 release with [TorchDynamo](/index.php?title=TorchDynamo&action=edit&redlink=1)

- **2025**: Used by approximately 80% of researchers at major ML conferences[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-pytorch-foundation-15)

### Large Language Models (LLaMA)

Meta AI has developed the [LLaMA](/wiki/llama) (Large Language Model Meta AI) series of [large language models](/index.php?title=Large_language_models&action=edit&redlink=1):

| Model | Release Date | Parameters | Key Features | License |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| LLaMA 1 | February 2023 | 7B-65B | Foundation models only | Non-commercial |
| LLaMA 2 | July 2023 | 7B-70B | Chat models included, commercial use allowed | Llama 2 Community License |
| LLaMA 3 | April 2024 | 8B-70B | Improved performance | Llama 3 Open License |
| LLaMA 3.1 | July 2024 | 8B-405B | Largest open-source model (405B) | Llama 3 Open License |
| LLaMA 3.2 | September 2024 | 1B-90B | Multimodal capabilities | Llama 3 Open License |
| LLaMA 3.3 | December 2024 | 70B | Optimized performance | Llama 3 Open License |
| LLaMA 4 Scout | April 2025 | 109B (17B active) | 10M token context, MoE architecture | Llama 4 Community License |
| LLaMA 4 Maverick | April 2025 | 400B (17B active) | 1M token context, 128 experts | Llama 4 Community License |
| LLaMA 4 Behemoth | In training | 2T (288B active) | 16 experts, STEM-focused | TBD |

[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-llama-wikipedia-16)[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-llama4-release-17)

LLaMA 4, released in April 2025, introduced Meta's first [mixture-of-experts](/index.php?title=Mixture-of-experts&action=edit&redlink=1) (MoE) architecture models and native [multimodality](/index.php?title=Multimodal_AI&action=edit&redlink=1), supporting text, image, and video inputs.[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-llama4-techcrunch-18) The unreleased Behemoth model, with 2 trillion total parameters, reportedly outperforms [GPT-4.5](/index.php?title=GPT-4.5&action=edit&redlink=1), [Claude](/wiki/claude) 3.7 Sonnet, and [Gemini](/index.php?title=Gemini&action=edit&redlink=1) 2.0 Pro on STEM benchmarks.[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-llama4-release-17)

### Computer Vision and Multimodal AI

Meta AI has made significant contributions to [computer vision](/wiki/computer_vision):

- **[Segment Anything Model](/wiki/segment_anything_model) (SAM)**: Released in April 2023, SAM segments any object in an image [zero-shot](/index.php?title=Zero-shot_learning&action=edit&redlink=1) and ships with the SA-1B dataset of one billion masks[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-sam-19)

- **[DINOv3](/index.php?title=DINOv3&action=edit&redlink=1)**: Released in August 2025, scales [self-supervised learning](/wiki/self-supervised_learning) for images to create universal vision backbones[&#91;20&#93;](#cite_note-dinov3-20)

- **[Detectron2](/index.php?title=Detectron2&action=edit&redlink=1)**: Open-source object detection library

- **Self-supervised learning techniques**: Development of methods that learn from unlabeled data

### Natural Language Processing

Meta AI's [NLP](/wiki/nlp) research focuses on:

- **[Machine translation](/index.php?title=Machine_translation&action=edit&redlink=1)**: Including [unsupervised machine translation](/index.php?title=Unsupervised_machine_translation&action=edit&redlink=1) techniques

- **Language understanding**: Developing systems for natural human-machine interactions

- **[Conversational AI](/index.php?title=Conversational_AI&action=edit&redlink=1)**: Creating more engaging and context-aware conversational agents[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-meta-ai-wiki-1)

### Game-Playing AI

- **[Cicero](/index.php?title=Cicero_(AI)&action=edit&redlink=1)**: Published in *[Science](/index.php?title=Science_(journal)&action=edit&redlink=1)* (December 2022), the first AI agent to reach human-level play in [Diplomacy](/index.php?title=Diplomacy_(game)&action=edit&redlink=1), integrating planning with LLMs to negotiate, ally and betray, doubling average human scores[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-cicero-21)

### Mathematical and Scientific AI

- **Protein Structure Prediction**: In 2022, Meta AI predicted the 3D shape of 600 million potential proteins in two weeks[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-meta-ai-wiki-1)

- **[HyperTree Proof Search](/index.php?title=HyperTree_Proof_Search&action=edit&redlink=1) (HTPS)**: AI system that proved 10 [International Mathematical Olympiad](/index.php?title=International_Mathematical_Olympiad&action=edit&redlink=1) problems in [Lean](/index.php?title=Lean_(proof_assistant)&action=edit&redlink=1) in 2022[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-math_proving-22)

- **[Galactica](/index.php?title=Galactica&action=edit&redlink=1)**: A large language model designed for scientific text generation released in November 2022, but withdrawn within days due to issues with offensiveness and inaccuracy[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-galactica-23)

## Meta AI Assistant

In addition to research, Meta AI also refers to an AI assistant developed by the division. The Meta AI assistant has been integrated across Meta's platforms:

### Platform Integration

The Meta AI assistant was rolled out across multiple platforms with the following timeline:

- **September 27, 2023**: Debuted as voice assistant on [Ray-Ban Meta](/index.php?title=Ray-Ban_Meta&action=edit&redlink=1) (2nd generation) [smart glasses](/index.php?title=Smart_glasses&action=edit&redlink=1)[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-meta-ai-wiki-1)

- **April 23, 2024**: Evolved to support multimodal input via [computer vision](/wiki/computer_vision)

- **July 23, 2024**: [Meta AI with Vision](/index.php?title=Meta_AI_with_Vision&action=edit&redlink=1) integrated into [Horizon OS](/index.php?title=Horizon_OS&action=edit&redlink=1) v68 on [Meta Quest 3](/index.php?title=Meta_Quest_3&action=edit&redlink=1) and [Quest Pro](/index.php?title=Quest_Pro&action=edit&redlink=1), made available on [Quest 2](/index.php?title=Quest_2&action=edit&redlink=1) without Vision[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-meta-ai-wiki-1)

- **April 29, 2025**: Standalone [Meta AI app](/index.php?title=Meta_AI_app&action=edit&redlink=1) launched with [Llama 4](/index.php?title=Llama_4&action=edit&redlink=1) integration[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-app_launch-24)

- **December 2024**: Reached nearly 600 million monthly active users[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-mau-600m-25)

Integration platforms include:

- [WhatsApp](/index.php?title=WhatsApp&action=edit&redlink=1)

- [Facebook](/index.php?title=Facebook&action=edit&redlink=1)

- [Meta Quest](/index.php?title=Meta_Quest&action=edit&redlink=1) [VR headsets](/index.php?title=VR_headset&action=edit&redlink=1)

### Features

The assistant offers various capabilities:

- Conversational AI powered by LLaMA models

- Image generation and editing

- Real-time information access (via [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1) and [Microsoft Bing](/index.php?title=Microsoft_Bing&action=edit&redlink=1))

- Multimodal input via computer vision (on supported devices)

- Voice interaction capabilities with [full-duplex](/index.php?title=Full-duplex&action=edit&redlink=1) speech technology

- "Discover feed" for sharing and exploring AI prompts[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-app_launch-24)

### User Customization

Users can mute the Meta AI assistant on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp, and hide AI elements via settings. However, the assistant cannot be completely disabled on all platforms.[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-meta-privacy-26)

## Open-Source Strategy

CEO [Mark Zuckerberg](/index.php?title=Mark_Zuckerberg&action=edit&redlink=1) argues that open models "make AI safer and more accessible," and Meta regularly releases code, weights and datasets under permissive terms.[&#91;32&#93;](#cite_note-open-32) This philosophy has led to:

- Open-sourcing PyTorch, now used by approximately 80% of researchers at major ML conferences[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-pytorch-foundation-15)

- Releasing LLaMA models under increasingly permissive licenses

- Publishing research papers and datasets like SA-1B (1 billion segmentation masks)

- Contributing libraries such as [FAISS](/index.php?title=FAISS&action=edit&redlink=1) and Detectron2 to the community

- Enabling developers to use LLaMA models for [synthetic data](/index.php?title=Synthetic_data&action=edit&redlink=1) generation

## Talent Acquisition and Competition

Meta has engaged in aggressive talent acquisition strategies to compete with rivals:

- Offered signing bonuses reportedly as high as $100 million to recruit from competitors[&#91;34&#93;](#cite_note-talent-war-34)

- [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) CEO [Sam Altman](/index.php?title=Sam_Altman&action=edit&redlink=1) confirmed Meta's recruitment attempts with large compensation packages[&#91;34&#93;](#cite_note-talent-war-34)

- Successfully recruited researchers from OpenAI, [Google DeepMind](/wiki/google_deepmind), and other leading labs

- CTO Andrew Bosworth acknowledged the "unprecedented" market rates for AI talent[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-msl-announcement-9)

## Impact and Significance

Meta AI has had substantial impact on the AI research community:

- **PyTorch Adoption**: Used by approximately 80% of researchers at major ML conferences[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-pytorch-foundation-15)

- **Open Source Philosophy**: Meta's commitment to open-source AI has democratized access to advanced models

- **LLaMA Models**: Provided alternatives to closed-source models from [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) and [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1)

- **Research Publications**: Hundreds of papers advancing the state-of-the-art in AI

- **Industry Influence**: Set standards for open AI research in corporate settings

- **User Reach**: Meta AI assistant used by nearly 600 million monthly active users as of December 2024[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-mau-600m-25)

## Criticism and Controversies

### Galactica Incident

In November 2022, Meta AI released Galactica, a large language model designed for generating scientific text. The model was withdrawn within three days due to issues with offensiveness, inaccuracy, and fabricated scientific content.[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-galactica-23)

### Copyright and Privacy Concerns

- Meta's use of public data for AI training has raised privacy concerns, particularly in the [European Union](/index.php?title=European_Union&action=edit&redlink=1) where users can opt out of data collection for AI purposes[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-meta-privacy-26)

- In May 2024, the Meta AI chatbot was summarizing news articles without providing direct links to original sources, raising ethical and legal concerns, particularly in Canada where news links are banned on Meta's platforms[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-meta-ai-wiki-1)

- Reports in 2025 suggested Meta may have used pirated books for training AI models, raising intellectual property concerns[&#91;35&#93;](#cite_note-piracy-concerns-35)

### Internal Tensions

- High-profile departures including Joelle Pineau raised questions about research direction[&#91;33&#93;](#cite_note-pineau-departure-33)

- Tension between fundamental research (FAIR) and product-focused development (GenAI team)[&#91;34&#93;](#cite_note-talent-war-34)

- Some insiders claimed in 2025 that the research lab is "dying a slow death" due to focus shift[&#91;36&#93;](#cite_note-fortune-dying-36)

### LLaMA 4 Reception

The April 2025 release of LLaMA 4 received mixed reactions:

- Allegations of inflated performance metrics

- Reports of a rushed release

- Concerns about transparency

- Competition from Chinese lab [DeepSeek](/wiki/deepseek)'s models[&#91;37&#93;](#cite_note-llama4-criticism-37)

Meta has denied claims of gaming performance metrics and attributed mixed performance to early bugs.[&#91;37&#93;](#cite_note-llama4-criticism-37)

### Regulatory and Safety Concerns

- In May 2025, Meta announced plans to replace human reviewers with AI systems for assessing privacy and societal risks, raising safety concerns among employees[&#91;38&#93;](#cite_note-ai-review-38)

- The company has faced scrutiny over content moderation approaches and AI safety protocols[&#91;35&#93;](#cite_note-piracy-concerns-35)

## Future Directions

Meta AI's stated goals include:

- Achieving "personal superintelligence for everyone" through MSL[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-msl-announcement-9)

- Developing AI agents capable of complex reasoning and action[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-llama4-techcrunch-18)

- Reaching one billion Meta AI chatbot users[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-mau-600m-25)

- Continuing investment in AI infrastructure with plans for multi-gigawatt data centers[&#91;28&#93;](#cite_note-infrastructure-2025-28)

- Advancing [AI glasses](/index.php?title=AI_glasses&action=edit&redlink=1) and [wearables](/index.php?title=Wearable_technology&action=edit&redlink=1) as ideal form factors for AI[&#91;29&#93;](#cite_note-capex-2026-29)

- Hosting [LlamaCon](/index.php?title=LlamaCon&action=edit&redlink=1), Meta's first AI developer conference (April 29, 2025)[&#91;39&#93;](#cite_note-llamacon-39)
