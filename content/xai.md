X.AI Corp.**, doing business as **xAI**, is an [American](/index.php?title=American&action=edit&redlink=1) company working in the area of [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) (AI), [machine learning](/wiki/machine_learning), and technology. Founded by [Elon Musk](/index.php?title=Elon_Musk&action=edit&redlink=1) in March 2023, the company develops AI systems with the stated mission to "understand the true nature of the universe."[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-mission-4) The company's flagship product is [Grok](/wiki/grok), a [generative AI](/index.php?title=Generative_artificial_intelligence&action=edit&redlink=1) [chatbot](/index.php?title=Chatbot&action=edit&redlink=1) integrated with the [X](/index.php?title=X_(social_media_platform)&action=edit&redlink=1) platform (formerly [Twitter](/index.php?title=Twitter&action=edit&redlink=1)).[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-grok-launch-5) In March 2025, xAI acquired [X Corp.](/index.php?title=X_Corp.&action=edit&redlink=1) in an all-stock transaction that valued xAI at $80 billion and X at $33 billion.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-acquisition-6)

## History

### Founding and Early Development

xAI was incorporated as X.AI Corp. on March 9, 2023, in [Nevada](/index.php?title=Nevada&action=edit&redlink=1), initially as a [public-benefit corporation](/index.php?title=Public-benefit_corporation&action=edit&redlink=1) with the stated purpose of "creating a material positive impact on society and the environment."[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-incorporation-7) [Elon Musk](/index.php?title=Elon_Musk&action=edit&redlink=1), who had previously co-founded [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) in 2015 but left in 2018 citing disagreements over its direction, recruited engineers and data scientists from leading AI companies including [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1), [Microsoft](/index.php?title=Microsoft&action=edit&redlink=1), [DeepMind](/index.php?title=DeepMind&action=edit&redlink=1), and OpenAI.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-founding-team-8)

Musk officially announced the formation of xAI on July 12, 2023, via [X](/index.php?title=X_(social_network)&action=edit&redlink=1), introducing a founding technical team and stating the company's goal to develop [Artificial general intelligence](/wiki/artificial_general_intelligence) (AGI) that is "maximally curious" and "maximally truth-seeking."[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-announcement-9)[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-MuskTweet-10)

By May 2024, xAI had dropped its public-benefit corporation status, as revealed by media reports in August 2025.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-benefit-status-11) In November 2023, Musk stated that investors in X Corp. would own 25% of xAI.[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-x-investors-12)

### Colossus Supercomputer

In June 2024, xAI announced plans to build [Colossus](/index.php?title=Colossus_(supercomputer)&action=edit&redlink=1), described as "the world's largest supercomputer," in [Memphis, Tennessee](/index.php?title=Memphis,_Tennessee&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-colossus-announce-19) The facility was constructed in a record 122 days at a former [Electrolux](/index.php?title=Electrolux&action=edit&redlink=1) site and became fully operational in December 2024.[&#91;20&#93;](#cite_note-colossus-operational-20) [Dell Technologies](/index.php?title=Dell_Technologies&action=edit&redlink=1) and [Supermicro](/index.php?title=Supermicro&action=edit&redlink=1) partnered with xAI to build the massive computing infrastructure.[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-dell-supermicro-21)

### Chip Diversion Controversy

In August 2024, Musk diverted [Nvidia](/wiki/nvidia) chips originally ordered for [Tesla, Inc.](/index.php?title=Tesla,_Inc.&action=edit&redlink=1) to xAI and X Corp., raising questions about conflicts of interest among Tesla shareholders.[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-chip-diversion-22)

### Government Contracts

In July 2025, xAI launched "Grok for Government" and secured a $200 million contract from the [United States Department of Defense](/index.php?title=United_States_Department_of_Defense&action=edit&redlink=1) for AI applications in the military, alongside [Anthropic](/wiki/anthropic), [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1), and OpenAI.[&#91;26&#93;](#cite_note-grok-government-26) The company's products became available through the [General Services Administration](/index.php?title=General_Services_Administration&action=edit&redlink=1) (GSA) schedule, with a OneGov agreement providing access to federal agencies for $0.42 per organization for 18 months.[&#91;27&#93;](#cite_note-gsa-schedule-27)

### Workforce Changes

In September 2025, xAI laid off 500 data annotation workers, approximately one-third of that team, as the company shifted focus to specialist roles.[&#91;28&#93;](#cite_note-layoffs-28) As of early 2025, xAI had over 1,200 employees, including 900 hourly-paid AI tutors for model training, though estimates vary with some sources reporting 700 to 4,000 employees.[&#91;29&#93;](#cite_note-employees-range-29)

## Products and Services

### Grok

Main article: [Grok (chatbot)and](/index.php?title=Grok_(chatbot)&action=edit&redlink=1) [[{{{2}}}]]}}}

Grok is xAI's flagship AI [chatbot](/index.php?title=Chatbot&action=edit&redlink=1), launched in November 2023. Named after the term from [Robert A. Heinlein](/index.php?title=Robert_A._Heinlein&action=edit&redlink=1)'s novel [Stranger in a Strange Land](/index.php?title=Stranger_in_a_Strange_Land&action=edit&redlink=1)*, Grok is designed to answer questions with wit and a rebellious personality, inspired by *[The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy](/index.php?title=The_Hitchhiker%27s_Guide_to_the_Galaxy&action=edit&redlink=1)*.[&#91;30&#93;](#cite_note-grok-inspiration-30) A key feature is its real-time access to information from the [X](/index.php?title=X_(social_network)&action=edit&redlink=1) platform.

### Aurora

[Aurora](/index.php?title=Aurora&action=edit&redlink=1) is xAI's [text-to-image model](/index.php?title=Text-to-image_model&action=edit&redlink=1), released on December 9, 2024. It is an autoregressive [mixture-of-experts](/index.php?title=Mixture-of-experts&action=edit&redlink=1) network trained on billions of examples from the internet, excelling at photorealistic rendering and following text instructions.[&#91;43&#93;](#cite_note-aurora-details-43) Aurora replaced an earlier partnership with [Black Forest Labs](/index.php?title=Black_Forest_Labs&action=edit&redlink=1)' Flux model.[&#91;44&#93;](#cite_note-flux-partnership-44)

### Platform and API

xAI launched its API on October 21, 2024, providing developers access to Grok models.[&#91;45&#93;](#cite_note-xai-api-45) The company also offers SuperGrok subscriptions for higher usage limits and access to advanced models, with Grok Heavy available at $300/month.[&#91;46&#93;](#cite_note-supergrok-46)

## Controversies

### Environmental Impact

The Colossus supercomputer has faced significant criticism for its environmental impact. In 2024, xAI deployed 14 portable methane-gas generators without permits, emitting pollutants equivalent to a nearby power plant.[&#91;82&#93;](#cite_note-environmental-82) Thermal imaging in May 2025 showed 33 generators operating. The [Shelby County Health Department](/index.php?title=Shelby_County_Health_Department&action=edit&redlink=1) granted xAI an air permit for the project in July 2025 after public outcry.[&#91;83&#93;](#cite_note-air-permit-83)

### Content Moderation Issues

Grok has generated controversy for producing inappropriate content, including:

- Antisemitic posts and praise of [Adolf Hitler](/index.php?title=Adolf_Hitler&action=edit&redlink=1) in July 2025[&#91;84&#93;](#cite_note-antisemitic-84)

- The chatbot referring to itself as "MechaHitler"[&#91;85&#93;](#cite_note-mechaHitler-85)

- Generating controversial images of public figures and copyrighted characters[&#91;86&#93;](#cite_note-image-controversy-86)

### Corporate Governance

The diversion of Nvidia chips from Tesla to xAI in August 2024 raised concerns about conflicts of interest and corporate governance among Tesla shareholders.[&#91;87&#93;](#cite_note-tesla-conflict-87)
