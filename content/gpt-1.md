GPT-1 is the first [GPT](/wiki/gpt) [model](/wiki/model) released by [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) in June 2018 with the [paper](/wiki/paper) [Improving Language Understanding by Generative Pre-Training](/wiki/improving_language_understanding_by_generative_pre-training). The developers found that combining the [transformer](/wiki/transformer) [architecture](/index.php?title=Architecture&action=edit&redlink=1) and [unsupervised pertaining](/index.php?title=Unsupervised_pertaining&action=edit&redlink=1) produced amazing results. GPT-1, according to the developers, was tailored for specific tasks in order to "strongly understand natural language."

GPT-1 was an important stepping stone toward a [language model](/wiki/language_model) that possesses general [language-based abilitiess](/index.php?title=Language-based_abilities&action=edit&redlink=1). It showed that language models can be efficiently [pre-trained](/index.php?title=Pre-trained&action=edit&redlink=1) which may help them generalize. The architecture was capable of performing many [NLP](/wiki/nlp) tasks with minimal [fine-tuning](/index.php?title=Fine-tuning&action=edit&redlink=1). GPT-1 used [BooksCorpus](/index.php?title=BooksCorpus&action=edit&redlink=1), which includes some 7,000 books. [Self-attention](/index.php?title=Self-attention&action=edit&redlink=1) was required in the transformer's coder to train the model. The architecture was identical to the original transformer and had 117 million [parameters](/index.php?title=Parameters&action=edit&redlink=1). This model was a precursor to the larger models (models with far more parameters and trained on larger [datasets](/wiki/datasets)) later on. Its notable ability was its performance on [zero shot](/index.php?title=Zero_shot&action=edit&redlink=1) [tasks](/index.php?title=Tasks&action=edit&redlink=1) in [natural language processing](/index.php?title=Natural_language_processing&action=edit&redlink=1) (for example [question-answering](/index.php?title=Question-answering&action=edit&redlink=1) or [sentiment analysis](/wiki/sentiment_analysis)) thanks to its [pre-training](/wiki/pre-training). [Zero-shot learning](/index.php?title=Zero-shot_learning&action=edit&redlink=1) means that a model can perform a task without seeing [examples](/wiki/examples). [Zero-shot task transfers](/index.php?title=Zero-shot_task_transfer&action=edit&redlink=1) is when the model is given very few examples. Instead, they must learn the task from the [instructions](/index.php?title=Instructions&action=edit&redlink=1) and very few examples.
