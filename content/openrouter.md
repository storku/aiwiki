**OpenRouter** is a unified [API](/index.php?title=API&action=edit&redlink=1) gateway platform and marketplace that provides developers with access to over 400 [large language models](/index.php?title=Large_language_models&action=edit&redlink=1) (LLMs) from multiple providers through a single, standardized interface.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-datacamp-1)[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-orrick-2) Founded in early 2023 by [Alex Atallah](/index.php?title=Alex_Atallah&action=edit&redlink=1), co-founder and former [CTO](/index.php?title=Chief_Technology_Officer&action=edit&redlink=1) of [OpenSea](/index.php?title=OpenSea&action=edit&redlink=1), and engineer Louis Vichy, the platform aims to simplify the integration and optimization of [AI](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) models while providing price transparency, reliability, and consolidated billing.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-developer-puter-3)[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-forbes-4)

## Overview

OpenRouter functions as an intermediary service and the first [LLM](/wiki/llm) marketplace, normalizing access to various AI models through a consistent [API](/index.php?title=API&action=edit&redlink=1) schema compatible with [OpenAI](/wiki/openai)'s Chat API.[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-techcrunch-5) This allows developers to switch between different [LLM](/wiki/llm) providers without changing their code implementation, addressing the problem of "API sprawl" where developers would otherwise need to maintain separate integrations for each model provider.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-developer-puter-3)[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-techcrunch-5)

The platform operates as a remote-first company headquartered in [New York City](/index.php?title=New_York_City&action=edit&redlink=1), with additional offices in [San Francisco, California](/index.php?title=San_Francisco,_California&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-orrick-2)[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-cbinsights-6) As of 2025, the company has 8 employees and serves over 2.5 million developers.[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7)

The service supports a growing catalog of over 400 models from 60+ providers including [OpenAI](/wiki/openai), [Anthropic](/wiki/anthropic), [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1), [Mistral AI](/wiki/mistral_ai), [Meta](/wiki/meta), [DeepSeek](/wiki/deepseek), [xAI](/wiki/xai), and various [open-source](/index.php?title=Open-source&action=edit&redlink=1) implementations.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-datacamp-1)[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7) The platform adds approximately 25-40 milliseconds of edge latency to end-to-end inference for most requests while providing automatic failover, load balancing, and unified billing.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-orrick-2)[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-openrouter-home-8)

## History

OpenRouter was founded in February/March 2023 by Alex Atallah, shortly after witnessing the emergence of [open-source](/index.php?title=Open-source&action=edit&redlink=1) LLMs like [Meta](/wiki/meta)'s [LLaMA](/wiki/llama).[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-developer-puter-3) The initial inspiration came after Atallah observed models like [Stanford](/index.php?title=Stanford_University&action=edit&redlink=1)'s Alpaca, which demonstrated that smaller teams could create competitive AI models with minimal resources. This suggested a future ecosystem with numerous specialized models, potentially requiring a marketplace to effectively navigate them.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-developer-puter-3)

Atallah, who previously co-founded the $14 billion [NFT](/index.php?title=NFT&action=edit&redlink=1) marketplace [OpenSea](/index.php?title=OpenSea&action=edit&redlink=1) and served as Director of Product & Engineering at [Kaggle](/index.php?title=Kaggle&action=edit&redlink=1), partnered with engineer Louis Vichy and one of his collaborators from the [browser extension](/index.php?title=Browser_extension&action=edit&redlink=1) framework Plasmo to launch OpenRouter.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-developer-puter-3)[&#91;9&#93;](#cite_note-crunchbase-9)[&#91;10&#93;](#cite_note-wsj-10)

In its early stages, OpenRouter processed approximately 10 trillion [tokens](/index.php?title=Tokenization_(artificial_intelligence)&action=edit&redlink=1) annually. By mid-2025, it had scaled to over 100 trillion tokens per year, representing a 10x growth.[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7) A key milestone was serving as the exclusive launch partner for [OpenAI](/wiki/openai)'s first coding model, GPT 4.1 (released as Quasar Alpha on the platform).[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7)

## Technical Implementation

### API Architecture

OpenRouter provides a single [API endpoint](/index.php?title=API_endpoint&action=edit&redlink=1) at `[https://openrouter.ai/api/v1`](https://openrouter.ai/api/v1`) that implements the [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) API specification for `/completions` and `/chat/completions` endpoints.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-api-overview-13) The platform normalizes request/response schemas across providers to reduce per-vendor integration work, while still allowing provider-specific options to pass through when needed.[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-api-params-14)

Key technical features include:

- [Bearer token](/index.php?title=Bearer_token&action=edit&redlink=1) authentication with [OAuth](/index.php?title=OAuth&action=edit&redlink=1) [PKCE](/index.php?title=PKCE&action=edit&redlink=1) support for user delegation[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-authentication-15)

- GitHub secret scanning partnership for exposed key detection[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-authentication-15)

- [SSE](/index.php?title=Server-sent_events&action=edit&redlink=1) streaming for real-time response handling[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-api-overview-13)

- Normalized token counting based on the GPT-4o tokenizer for consistency[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-api-overview-13)

### Supported Parameters

The API supports comprehensive parameters for model configuration:[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-api-params-14)

| Parameter | Description | Range/Values |
| --- | --- | --- |
| temperature | Controls response randomness | 0-2 |
| max_tokens | Maximum response length | Model-dependent |
| top_p | Nucleus sampling threshold | 0-1 |
| frequency_penalty | Reduces repetition of frequent tokens | -2 to 2 |
| presence_penalty | Reduces repetition of any used tokens | -2 to 2 |
| stream | Enables [SSE](/index.php?title=Server-sent_events&action=edit&redlink=1) streaming | true/false |
| tools | Function calling configuration | JSON schema |
| response_format | Enforces structured output | JSON schema |

### Provider Routing

OpenRouter employs intelligent routing algorithms that consider multiple factors:[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-provider-routing-16)

| Routing Method | Identifier Suffix | Description | Use Case |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Default | (none) | Load balances across providers, prioritizing price | General purpose |
| Nitro | :nitro | Optimizes for throughput and response speed | Time-sensitive applications |
| Floor | :floor | Prioritizes the lowest cost options | Budget-conscious deployments |
| Online | :online | Includes web search results via [Exa.ai](/index.php?title=Exa.ai&action=edit&redlink=1) | Real-time information needs |
| Auto | openrouter/auto | AI-powered model selection using [NotDiamond](/index.php?title=NotDiamond&action=edit&redlink=1) | Automatic optimization |
| Custom | User-defined | Specific provider preferences | Enterprise requirements |

The system automatically falls back to alternative providers when the primary endpoint returns errors or exceeds latency thresholds, improving overall reliability to maintain uptime.[&#91;16&#93;](#cite_note-provider-routing-16)[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-faq-17)

### Multimodal Support

The platform supports multimodal inputs where the underlying models allow it:[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-datacamp-1)

- Image processing and analysis with vision-capable models

- [PDF](/index.php?title=PDF&action=edit&redlink=1) document parsing with [OCR](/index.php?title=OCR&action=edit&redlink=1) capabilities

- Audio input processing for transcription and analysis

- Structured data extraction from documents

- Video processing support (planned as of 2025)

### Web Search Integration

OpenRouter offers web search augmentation through integration with [Exa.ai](/index.php?title=Exa.ai&action=edit&redlink=1). Appending `:online` to any model identifier enables real-time web search capabilities, with retrieved information injected into the model context with proper citations. This feature is priced at $4 per 1,000 search results.[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-web-search-18)

### Advanced Capabilities

- **Structured Outputs**: JSON schema enforcement via `response_format` parameter for predictable parsing[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-api-params-14)

- **Tool/Function Calling**: Support for parallel tool calls with `tools` and `tool_choice` parameters[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-api-params-14)

- **Context Caching**: Optimization for repeated prompts to reduce costs and latency[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-datacamp-1)

- **Reasoning Tokens**: Reveals model thinking processes for complex tasks with configurable token budgets[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-datacamp-1)

- **Community Leaderboards**: Public rankings showing model usage statistics, performance metrics, and user votes[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7)[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-openrouter-home-8)

### Analytics and Observability

OpenRouter provides comprehensive analytics dashboards showing:[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-api-overview-13)

- Token consumption and costs across models

- Latency metrics and performance trends

- Error rates and failure analysis

- Usage patterns by application, team, or API key

- Real-time monitoring and status updates

The platform also offers programmatic access to usage data via the `/api/v1/generation` endpoint for integration with custom monitoring systems.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-api-overview-13)

## Pricing and Billing

### Cost Structure

OpenRouter uses a [credit](/index.php?title=Credit&action=edit&redlink=1)-based prepaid system with pass-through pricing:[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-faq-17)[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7)

- **Model Costs**: Direct per-token charges from underlying providers, billed separately for input and output tokens

- **Platform Fee**: Approximately 5% cut of inference costs as primary revenue[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7)

- **Credit Purchase Fee**: 5.5% fee (minimum $0.80) for credit card purchases, or 5.0% for cryptocurrency payments in [USDC](/index.php?title=USD_Coin&action=edit&redlink=1)[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-faq-17)

Example model pricing (as of October 2025):[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-datacamp-1)[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-openrouter-home-8)

Credits are purchased in bundles (for example $10 for 10 credits) with no expiration date, and volume discounts are available for enterprise customers.[&#91;8&#93;](#cite_note-openrouter-home-8)

### BYOK (Bring Your Own Keys)

For enterprise users with existing provider relationships, OpenRouter supports BYOK:[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-byok-19)

- First 1 million BYOK requests per month are free

- Subsequent requests incur a 5% fee of the normal OpenRouter cost

- Support for keys from [Azure](/index.php?title=Microsoft_Azure&action=edit&redlink=1), [AWS](/index.php?title=Amazon_Web_Services&action=edit&redlink=1) Bedrock, [Google Vertex AI](/index.php?title=Google_Cloud_Platform&action=edit&redlink=1), and other providers

- Automatic fallback to OpenRouter credits if user keys encounter issues

### Free Tier

OpenRouter offers a free tier with the following limits:[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-faq-17)[&#91;20&#93;](#cite_note-rate-limits-20)

- Users with less than 10 credits: 50 free model requests per day

- Users with 10+ credits: 1,000 free model requests per day

- Free model variants (`:free`) limited to 20 requests per minute

- Access to selected free models including DeepSeek Coder, Llama variants, and Devstral Small

## Privacy and Security

### Data Retention

OpenRouter's default privacy configuration includes:[&#91;17&#93;](#cite_note-faq-17)

- Prompt and response content storage disabled by default

- Only request metadata (timestamps, model used, token counts, latency) retained

- Opt-in prompt logging available, sometimes with associated discounts for model improvement

### Zero Data Retention (ZDR) Routing

Users can configure requests to route only to providers with verified zero data retention policies. This feature may limit model availability and potentially affect latency or pricing but provides enhanced privacy guarantees for sensitive applications.[&#91;1&#93;](#cite_note-datacamp-1)

### Compliance

As of July 2025, OpenRouter has achieved [SOC 2](/index.php?title=SOC_2&action=edit&redlink=1) Type I compliance and maintains a public trust portal detailing security practices and compliance status.[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-soc2-21) The platform implements [Cloudflare](/index.php?title=Cloudflare&action=edit&redlink=1) [DDoS](/index.php?title=DDoS&action=edit&redlink=1) protection and rate limiting for security.[&#91;20&#93;](#cite_note-rate-limits-20)

## Business Model and Market Position

### Revenue and Growth

According to company statements, OpenRouter's business model centers on:[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7)

- 5% cut of inference costs as primary revenue source

- Projected $25 billion AI inference market in 2025

- $15 billion addressable market from third-party applications

Co-founder Chris Clark stated: "We believe that inference costs will eclipse salaries as the dominant operating expense for most knowledge-based companies over the next five to 10 years."[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7)

The platform has facilitated significant market transparency in AI model pricing and performance. Market share data from August 2025 reveals [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1) (22.5%) and [Anthropic](/wiki/anthropic) (22.3%) as leading providers, followed by OpenAI and various open-source providers.[&#91;27&#93;](#cite_note-market-data-27)

### Competition

OpenRouter operates in the LLM benchmarking and model routing market.[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-cbinsights-6) Major competitors include:[&#91;28&#93;](#cite_note-tracxn-28)

- [Together AI](/index.php?title=Together_AI&action=edit&redlink=1) - Focus on open-source models and fine-tuning

- [AWS Bedrock](/index.php?title=AWS_Bedrock&action=edit&redlink=1) - Deep AWS integration for enterprise

- [Helicone](/index.php?title=Helicone&action=edit&redlink=1) - Observability-focused gateway

- Stability AI, LangChain, Fetch.ai, Lamini, MI2.ai

### User Base

As of 2025, OpenRouter serves:[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7)

- Over 2.5 million developers

- Applications processing 100+ trillion tokens annually

- Notable integrations including Cline and native [Visual Studio Code](/index.php?title=Visual_Studio_Code&action=edit&redlink=1) support

The platform has received endorsements from prominent figures in the AI community, including [Andrej Karpathy](/index.php?title=Andrej_Karpathy&action=edit&redlink=1), who highlighted its LLM rankings during a talk at [Y Combinator](/index.php?title=Y_Combinator&action=edit&redlink=1) Startup School on June 17, 2025.[&#91;7&#93;](#cite_note-menlo-7)

## Principles and Philosophy

OpenRouter's stated principles emphasize a multi-model and multi-provider future for AI development.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-developer-puter-3) The company highlights several key value propositions:

- Model diversity and choice for developers

- Price transparency without hidden markups on inference costs

- Reliability through automatic failover and load balancing

- Simplified integration across multiple providers

- Support for both commercial and open-source models

- Democratization of AI access through unified interfaces
