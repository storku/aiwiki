**Perplexity AI, Inc.** is an American [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) company that develops an AI-powered answer engine and search platform. Founded in August 2022 by Aravind Srinivas, Denis Yarats, Johnny Ho, and Andy Konwinski, the company launched its primary search product on December 7, 2022. Perplexity combines web search with [large language model](/wiki/large_language_model) (LLM) answer synthesis to provide direct, cited responses to user queries, positioning itself as an alternative to traditional search engines like [Google](/index.php?title=Google_Search&action=edit&redlink=1) Search. Headquartered in San Francisco, California, Perplexity AI had raised over $1.2 billion in total funding and reached a valuation exceeding $20 billion by late 2025.

## Overview

Perplexity AI operates as a conversational search engine, sometimes described as an "answer engine," that retrieves real-time information from the web and synthesizes it into direct, source-cited responses using [large language models](/wiki/large_language_model). Unlike traditional search engines that return lists of links, Perplexity generates natural language answers grounded in web sources, with inline citations allowing users to verify the information.

The platform is built on a [retrieval-augmented generation](/wiki/retrieval-augmented_generation) (RAG) architecture. When a user submits a query, the system first parses the user's intent using an LLM, then retrieves relevant documents from the web through a hybrid retrieval pipeline combining lexical and semantic search methods, and finally passes the retrieved content to a language model that generates a synthesized answer with references. This approach allows Perplexity to provide responses that are both conversational and factually grounded in current web content.

Perplexity offers its core search product for free without requiring registration, while a paid tier called Perplexity Pro provides access to more advanced AI models, deeper research capabilities, and additional features. The company also offers an API for developers, an enterprise product for businesses, and the Comet web browser with integrated AI capabilities.

Perplexity AI was founded in August 2022 by four co-founders with backgrounds at leading technology and AI companies:

The founding team drew on their collective experience in AI research, machine learning infrastructure, and search systems. Aravind Srinivas, the public face of the company, had worked extensively on problems related to search and information retrieval during his time at OpenAI and Google Brain. Denis Yarats brought deep expertise in AI research from Meta's AI labs. Johnny Ho contributed experience in both consumer internet products (from Quora) and quantitative systems. Andy Konwinski, as a co-founder of Databricks, had extensive experience building data and AI infrastructure at scale.

The company's search engine launched publicly on December 7, 2022, initially as a simple interface where users could ask questions and receive AI-generated answers with source citations. By February 2023, Perplexity had attracted two million unique visitors, demonstrating early product-market fit in the emerging AI search category.

### Chrome Acquisition Bid

In August 2025, Perplexity made headlines with an unsolicited $34.5 billion bid to acquire [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1)'s Chrome browser. The offer, dubbed "Project Solomon" internally, came in the context of the U.S. Department of Justice's antitrust case against Google, in which a federal court ruled that Google had acted as an illegal monopoly in search. The DOJ proposed forcing Google to divest Chrome as a potential remedy.

In a letter accompanying the bid, CEO Aravind Srinivas stated that the offer was "designed to satisfy an antitrust remedy in highest public interest by placing Chrome with a capable, independent operator." Perplexity committed to investing $3 billion over 24 months in Chrome's reliability, performance, security, and customer support. Several investors agreed to back the $34.5 billion offer in full. However, Google did not indicate any willingness to sell Chrome, and CEO Sundar Pichai testified that a forced divestiture would harm Google's business. Industry observers characterized the bid as a long shot but noted it underscored Perplexity's ambitions and the shifting dynamics in the search market.

## Products and Services

### Perplexity Search (Free Tier)

The core Perplexity product is a free AI-powered search engine accessible via the web at perplexity.ai, as well as through iOS and Android mobile apps. Users can ask questions in natural language and receive direct answers with inline citations linking to source material. The free tier uses Perplexity's proprietary Sonar model (built on [Meta](/index.php?title=Meta_Platforms&action=edit&redlink=1)'s [Llama](/wiki/llama) 3.3 70B) as its default engine.

Key features of the free tier include:

- Natural language question answering with source citations
- Real-time web search integration
- Follow-up question suggestions for deeper exploration
- Up to three Spaces for organizing research
- Light file upload capabilities
- Limited access to Deep Research

### Perplexity Pro

Perplexity Pro is the premium subscription tier, priced at $20 per month (or $200 per year). It provides access to a broader set of AI models and more powerful research tools.

| Feature | Free | Pro ($20/month) | Max ($200/month) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Default model | Sonar | Choice of models | Unlimited model access |
| Pro Search | Limited | Extended daily quota | Unlimited |
| Deep Research | Limited | Full access | Full access |
| File uploads | Light | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Spaces | Up to 3 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| API credits | None | $5/month included | Included |
| Model selection | Sonar only | GPT-4o, [Claude](/wiki/claude), [Gemini](/index.php?title=Gemini_(language_model)&action=edit&redlink=1), Grok, Sonar Pro | All available models |

Pro subscribers can switch between multiple underlying AI models within the same interface, including models from [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) (GPT-4o and successors), [Anthropic](/wiki/anthropic) ([Claude](/wiki/claude) Sonnet), [Google](/index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1) ([Gemini](/index.php?title=Gemini_(language_model)&action=edit&redlink=1)), xAI (Grok), and Perplexity's own Sonar Pro. This multi-model approach lets users select the best model for different types of queries.

### Pro Search and Deep Research

Pro Search is Perplexity's advanced search mode that performs multi-step reasoning and analysis. Rather than answering a query in a single pass, Pro Search breaks complex questions into sub-queries, retrieves information from multiple angles, and synthesizes a more comprehensive response.

Deep Research takes this further, performing extended multi-step analysis tasks. Introduced in April 2025 and integrated into Sonar's architecture, Deep Research can execute 20 to 50 targeted queries per research session, clustering results by relevance and recency. A single Deep Research report can draw from over 200 sources, producing comprehensive analysis that would take a human researcher significantly longer to compile manually.

### Perplexity Pages

Launched in June 2024, Perplexity Pages is a content creation and publishing tool that transforms research into formatted articles, reports, and guides. Users can either convert an existing Perplexity conversation into a Page or create one from scratch by specifying a topic and target audience. Pages include:

- Structured content with titles, subheadings, and sections
- Associated media and relevant citations
- Customizable tone (general audience or expert-level)
- One-click publishing to Perplexity's library of user-generated content
- Direct sharing capabilities

Pages was designed for educators creating study guides, researchers writing up findings, and individuals sharing knowledge on specific topics. As of early 2026, the "Create page" feature was temporarily retired for enhancement, while the "Convert to Page" feature was set to return with improved capabilities.

### Perplexity Spaces

Spaces is an organizational feature that allows users to create persistent workspaces for ongoing research projects. Each Space maintains context across multiple queries and conversations, enabling users to build up knowledge on a topic over time. Free users can create up to three Spaces, while Pro subscribers have unlimited access.

### Comet Browser

Comet is Perplexity's AI-integrated web browser, designed to serve as a personal AI assistant for browsing, research, and task automation. Key details:

| Aspect | Details |
| --- | --- |

| Android release | November 20, 2025 |
| iOS release | March 18, 2026 |

Comet's features include:

- AI-powered search and browsing integrated directly into the browser
- Tab organization and management
- Email drafting assistance
- Shopping and product research with integrated checkout

Revenue from Comet Plus subscriptions is split, with 80% going to participating publishers and 20% retained by Perplexity.

**Comet Enterprise** extends the browser to business environments with enterprise-grade security, administrative controls, and compliance features for organizations looking to integrate AI browsing into their workflows.

### Perplexity Assistant

Launched in January 2025, the Perplexity Assistant is an AI-powered tool designed to extend the search engine's capabilities into task execution across multiple apps. The Assistant can perform actions such as hailing a ride, searching for a song, or managing device functions. It is multi-modal, meaning it can use a phone's camera to provide answers about the user's surroundings or on-screen content, and it maintains context across actions for a seamless experience.

### Shopping and Agentic Commerce

Perplexity has expanded into e-commerce with its "Buy with Pro" feature, initially launched in November 2024 for paid subscribers and expanded to all U.S. users in November 2025. The feature enables users to discover, research, and purchase products directly within the Perplexity interface.

Perplexity partnered with [PayPal](/index.php?title=PayPal&action=edit&redlink=1) in May 2025 to power agentic commerce, allowing users to check out instantly with PayPal or Venmo. Additional integrations with Shopify and BigCommerce enable purchasing from over 5,000 merchants. The company also integrated Firmly.ai's agentic commerce technology to enable the full shopping journey from discovery to checkout within the platform.

## Technical Architecture

### Retrieval-Augmented Generation Pipeline

Perplexity's core technical capability is not a single LLM but rather the orchestration of multiple language models with a high-performance search and retrieval system. The architecture is built around a [retrieval-augmented generation](/wiki/retrieval-augmented_generation) (RAG) pipeline that executes for nearly every query.

The RAG pipeline operates in several stages:

1. **Query Understanding:** An LLM parses the user's intent, going beyond simple keyword matching to understand the underlying question and any implicit context.
2. **Query Decomposition:** For complex questions, the system breaks the query into sub-questions that can be researched independently.
3. **Document Retrieval:** A hybrid retrieval pipeline combines lexical methods (keyword matching) with semantic indexing and [vector embeddings](/index.php?title=Vector_embedding&action=edit&redlink=1) to find the most relevant web content.
4. **Content Ranking:** Retrieved documents are ranked by relevance, recency, and source authority.
5. **Answer Synthesis:** The LLM generates a coherent response by weaving together its pre-trained knowledge and the freshly retrieved information.
6. **Citation Generation:** The system adds inline references to source documents, enabling users to verify claims.

### Search Infrastructure

Rather than relying on a static search index like traditional search engines, Perplexity uses on-demand crawling and API ingestion to access up-to-date content. The company uses [Vespa.ai](https://vespa.ai/) as the backbone of its search infrastructure. Vespa's serving engine combines distributed retrieval, ranking, and machine learning inference in a single low-latency pipeline, handling thousands of concurrent hybrid queries per second while maintaining sub-second response times.

### Models

Perplexity operates through a multi-model architecture, dynamically routing queries to different engines depending on the task type (conversational, research, coding, or enterprise). The system uses small, efficient classifier models to determine query intent and complexity before selecting the appropriate model.

**Proprietary Models:**

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

| Sonar Pro | Proprietary fine-tune | March 2025 | 200K tokens | Advanced search and analysis |
| Sonar Deep Research | Multi-model orchestration | April 2025 | Extended | Multi-step research with 20-50 targeted queries |

**Third-Party Models Available (Pro tier):**

| Provider | Models Available |

| [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) | [GPT-4o](/wiki/gpt-4), GPT-4 Turbo, and successors |

Perplexity's proprietary routing layer determines which model handles each query, balancing speed, accuracy, and cost. Free users typically interact with a blend of Sonar (based on Mistral and Gemini for some tasks), while Pro and Deep Research subscribers can access reasoning-heavy models like GPT-4o and Claude.

## Business Model

Perplexity operates on a freemium model with multiple revenue streams:

### Subscription Revenue

The primary revenue source is subscriptions across three tiers:

| Tier | Price | Target Users |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Free | $0 | General users; basic search with Sonar |
| Pro | $20/month ($200/year) | Power users; advanced models and research tools |
| Max | $200/month | Heavy users; unlimited access to all models and features |
| Comet Plus | $5/month | Browser users; access to premium publisher content |
| Enterprise | $40/user/month | Organizations; security, compliance, and admin tools |

### Advertising

Perplexity introduced advertising through its related questions feature. Brands can pay to sponsor specific follow-up questions that appear after a user's initial query. When Perplexity earns advertising revenue from an interaction where a publisher's content is referenced, that publisher shares in the ad revenue through the Publishers' Program.

### API Revenue

The API operates on a pay-as-you-go model based on token usage and search depth. Developers pay per million input and output tokens, with pricing varying by model tier.

### Commerce

Through its shopping integrations with PayPal, Shopify, and BigCommerce, Perplexity earns revenue from e-commerce transactions completed within its platform.

### Publishers' Program

Perplexity launched its Publishers' Program in July 2024 to address concerns from content creators about the use of their material. Under the program, publishers who opt in share in revenue generated from subscriptions and advertising when their content is cited. In August 2025, Perplexity announced a $42.5 million revenue-sharing commitment to publishers through the Comet Plus program, with an 80/20 split favoring publishers.

Participating publishers include Fortune, Time, Der Spiegel, Le Monde, Los Angeles Times, ADWEEK, DPReview, Gear Patrol, The Independent, Gannett, Blavity, Lee Enterprises, and others.

### Competitive Positioning

Perplexity differentiates itself from competitors in several ways:

**Versus Google Search:** Google returns ranked lists of web links, while Perplexity provides synthesized answers with citations. Google's AI Overviews feature (introduced in 2024) represents the search giant's response to the AI answer engine trend. Analysis shows that Perplexity's cited sources have a 91% overlap with Google's top-10 search results, suggesting Perplexity effectively distills the same high-quality sources into a more direct answer format.

**Versus [ChatGPT](/wiki/chatgpt):** ChatGPT is stronger at creative writing, coding, and open-ended reasoning tasks, while Perplexity excels at factual research and information retrieval with source citations. ChatGPT's search feature has grown more capable, but Perplexity's purpose-built search infrastructure and consistent citation format give it an edge for research workflows. Many power users subscribe to both services, using Perplexity for fact-finding and ChatGPT for creative and analytical work.

**Versus Microsoft Copilot/Bing:** Bing integrates GPT-4 into a traditional search interface, while Perplexity offers a clean, focused answer engine experience. Perplexity's interface is simpler and more conversational, though Bing benefits from deeper integration with Microsoft's ecosystem.

## Controversies

### Publisher Disputes and Copyright Lawsuits

Perplexity has faced significant controversy over its use of publisher content. Multiple media organizations have filed lawsuits or issued legal threats alleging that Perplexity scraped and reproduced their content without authorization.

**Major legal actions include:**

| Date | Plaintiff(s) | Allegations |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 2024 | Forbes, Wired | Republishing proprietary content without proper citation |
| 2024 | BBC | Scraping and verbatim use of content without consent |
| 2025 | Nikkei, Asahi Shimbun (Japan) | Copying and storing article content; ignoring technical safeguards |
| September 2025 | Encyclopedia Britannica, Merriam-Webster | Unauthorized crawling and scraping of publications for RAG services |
| December 2025 | Chicago Tribune | Copyright infringement |
| December 2025 | The New York Times | Unauthorized use of content despite 18 months of licensing negotiations |

### Web Scraping Allegations

A 2024 investigation by Wired found evidence that Perplexity may have violated its stated commitment to respect robots.txt protocols. The report indicated that PerplexityBot used undisclosed IP addresses to access content from websites that had explicitly opted out of being scraped. Cloudflare separately alleged that Perplexity bypassed websites' no-crawling requests by disguising its crawler's identity.

### Response to Controversies

Perplexity has taken several steps to address publisher concerns:

- Launched the Publishers' Program in July 2024, offering revenue sharing with content creators whose work is cited in Perplexity responses.
- Committed $42.5 million in publisher payouts through the Comet Plus program.
- Signed revenue-sharing partnerships with outlets including Fortune, Time, Le Monde, Der Spiegel, the Los Angeles Times, and others.
- Introduced Comet Plus as a subscription tier with an 80/20 revenue split favoring publishers.

Despite these efforts, new lawsuits have continued to be filed. The tension between AI search companies and publishers mirrors the broader conflict between the AI industry and content creators over the use of copyrighted material for training and retrieval purposes.
