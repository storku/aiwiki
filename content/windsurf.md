**Windsurf** (formerly **Codeium**, originally **Exafunction**) is an AI-native [integrated development environment](/wiki/integrated_development_environment) (IDE) designed to deeply integrate [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) into every part of the software development workflow. Built on top of the [Visual Studio Code](/wiki/visual_studio_code) architecture, Windsurf combines agentic AI capabilities, intelligent code completion, and multi-file editing into a standalone desktop application. The product's signature feature, Cascade, functions as an autonomous AI coding agent that can understand entire codebases, suggest edits across multiple files, execute terminal commands, and interact with browsers, all while maintaining awareness of the developer's real-time actions and intent.

The company was founded in June 2021 by Varun Mohan and Douglas Chen as Exafunction, a GPU optimization startup. After pivoting to AI-powered developer tools in 2022 under the name Codeium, the team launched the Windsurf Editor in November 2024 and formally rebranded the entire company to Windsurf in April 2025. The startup raised approximately $243 million in total funding across multiple rounds, reaching a $1.25 billion valuation at its Series C in August 2024.

In a dramatic sequence of events in mid-2025, [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) agreed to acquire Windsurf for $3 billion, but the deal collapsed when the exclusivity period expired. [Google](/wiki/google) then hired Windsurf's co-founders and key researchers in a $2.4 billion reverse-acquihire, and Cognition (the company behind the AI coding agent Devin) subsequently acquired Windsurf's remaining IP, product, brand, and 210 employees. As of early 2026, Windsurf operates under Cognition's ownership and ranks first in the LogRocket AI Dev Tool Power Rankings.

## Company History

### Founding as Exafunction (2021)

Windsurf traces its origins to June 2021, when Varun Mohan and Douglas Chen co-founded a startup called Exafunction. Mohan and Chen first met in middle school and later became classmates at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). Before founding the company, Mohan served as a tech lead at Nuro, where he managed large-scale [deep learning](/wiki/deep_learning) infrastructure for [autonomous vehicles](/wiki/autonomous_vehicles). Chen worked at [Meta](/wiki/meta_platforms) developing software tools for VR headsets, including the Oculus Quest.

Exafunction focused on GPU optimization and virtualization for AI workloads. The original goal was to reduce AI development costs by abstracting away hardware complexity, enabling companies to run their [machine learning](/wiki/machine_learning) models more efficiently on distributed GPU clusters.

### Pivot to Codeium (2022)

As [generative AI](/wiki/generative_ai) models began to dominate the technology landscape in 2022, Mohan and Chen recognized that the next wave of innovation would not come solely from infrastructure optimization. Instead, they saw an opportunity to leverage that infrastructure knowledge to create end-user applications that directly enhanced developer productivity. This realization led to a strategic pivot: the company rebranded from Exafunction to Codeium and shifted its focus to building AI-powered coding extensions.

Codeium initially launched as a free AI code completion tool, competing with [GitHub Copilot](/wiki/github_copilot) by offering unlimited autocomplete suggestions at no cost. The product supported over 70 programming languages and was available as an extension across more than 40 IDEs, including Visual Studio Code, JetBrains IDEs, Vim, Neovim, and Emacs. By early 2023, Codeium had attracted roughly 10,000 users. By early 2025, the platform had grown to over 800,000 active developers, and the user base exceeded one million by April 2025.

### Launch of Windsurf Editor (November 2024)

In November 2024, Codeium launched the Windsurf Editor, its own standalone IDE that went beyond the extension model. Rather than simply augmenting existing editors with AI features, Windsurf was designed from the ground up as an AI-native development environment. The editor was built on VS Code's open-source foundation but deeply integrated AI capabilities into its core experience, including the Cascade agentic assistant, intelligent autocomplete, and multi-file editing workflows.

The launch marked a significant strategic shift. While Codeium's extensions continued to be available for other editors, the Windsurf Editor represented the company's vision for the future of software development: an environment where AI is not an add-on but a first-class participant in the coding process.

### Rebranding to Windsurf (April 2025)

In April 2025, the company officially rebranded from Codeium to Windsurf. The name change reflected the broader transformation of the company's identity, from a code autocomplete plugin provider to a full AI-native IDE company. The Windsurf brand was chosen to evoke the concept of "flow state," the mental state of deep, effortless focus where developer productivity is highest. The product's design philosophy centers on minimizing context switching and helping developers achieve and maintain this flow state.

## Acquisitions and Corporate Transactions (2025)

The mid-2025 period brought a rapid and unprecedented sequence of corporate transactions that resulted in Windsurf being effectively split among three major technology companies within 72 hours.

### OpenAI Acquisition Attempt

In April 2025, reports emerged that [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) was in talks to acquire Windsurf for approximately $3 billion, which would have been OpenAI's largest acquisition to date. OpenAI had reportedly also pursued Windsurf's competitor Cursor, but the two companies could not agree on a price. On May 6, 2025, Bloomberg reported that OpenAI had reached a definitive agreement to buy Windsurf for $3 billion.

The proposed acquisition was widely viewed as a strategic move by OpenAI to expand beyond its core [large language model](/wiki/large_language_model) business and into the developer tools space, where AI-assisted coding was becoming one of the fastest-growing segments in enterprise software. However, the deal included an exclusivity period during which Windsurf could not negotiate with other potential buyers.

### Deal Collapse and Google's Reverse-Acquihire

On July 11, 2025, the exclusivity period for the OpenAI deal expired without the acquisition closing. Within hours, Google announced a $2.4 billion deal to hire key Windsurf leadership and license the company's technology. This transaction was structured as a reverse-acquihire: Google did not acquire the company itself or take a stake in it. Instead, Google hired Windsurf CEO Varun Mohan, co-founder Douglas Chen, and approximately 40 of the startup's top researchers and engineers, who joined [Google DeepMind](/wiki/google_deepmind). As part of the arrangement, Google received a nonexclusive license to certain Windsurf technology.

The $2.4 billion figure covered both licensing fees and compensation packages for the departing employees. Windsurf's venture capital investors were also paid as part of the deal structure.

### Cognition's Acquisition

On July 14, 2025, just three days after the Google deal, Cognition (the company behind the autonomous AI coding agent Devin) signed a definitive agreement to acquire what remained of Windsurf. The acquisition, which closed in December 2025 for approximately $250 million, included:

- Windsurf's intellectual property, product, trademark, and brand
- Approximately 210 employees
- The company's $82 million in annual recurring revenue (ARR)
- Over 350 enterprise customers
- Hundreds of thousands of daily active users

Cognition committed to honoring all existing Windsurf customer agreements and employee benefits. One hundred percent of Windsurf employees participated financially in the deal, had their vesting cliffs waived, and received fully accelerated vesting for their work to date.

Following the acquisition, Cognition's combined annual recurring revenue more than doubled, and the company was subsequently valued at $10.2 billion in September 2025, approximately two months after completing the Windsurf purchase.

## Core Features

### Cascade

Cascade is Windsurf's flagship agentic AI assistant and the central feature that differentiates the IDE from traditional code editors. Cascade operates in two primary modes: a Code mode for making direct changes to the codebase and a Chat mode for answering questions and providing guidance without modifying files.

**Key capabilities of Cascade include:**

- **Multi-file editing:** Cascade can reason across an entire codebase and make coordinated changes across multiple files simultaneously. It understands project architecture, identifies where changes need to happen, and executes them without requiring the developer to specify each file individually.

- **Terminal command execution:** Cascade can run terminal commands autonomously, including installing packages, running build scripts, and executing tests. It asks for the developer's approval before running commands and prompts follow-up questions to ensure goals are achieved.

- **Real-time awareness:** Cascade tracks the developer's actions in real time, including edits, terminal commands, conversation history, clipboard contents, and more. This continuous awareness allows it to infer intent and adapt its suggestions without requiring the developer to repeat context.

- **Linter integration:** Cascade integrates with code linters to automatically detect and fix issues as they arise during the development process.

- **Tool calling:** Cascade can invoke external tools and APIs, read browser tabs, take screenshots, and incorporate console logs from running applications.

- **Voice input:** Developers can interact with Cascade using voice commands, enabling hands-free coding workflows.

- **Checkpoints:** Cascade creates automatic checkpoints at each step of its work, allowing developers to review changes incrementally and roll back to any previous state. Named checkpoints can be created to snapshot major milestones.

### AI Flows

Cascade introduces a concept called AI Flows, which represents a fundamentally different approach to human-AI collaboration in coding. Rather than treating AI as a simple autocomplete or chatbot, Flows enable a continuous, collaborative workflow where the AI generates or modifies code, asks for approval before executing commands, and then prompts follow-up questions to ensure the developer's objectives are fully met.

The Flow paradigm is designed to keep developers in a state of productive focus by minimizing the number of context switches required. Instead of jumping between a chat interface, a file editor, a terminal, and documentation, developers can stay within a single unified workflow.

### Windsurf Tab (Autocomplete and Supercomplete)

Windsurf Tab is the product's intelligent code completion system, which has evolved from a simple autocomplete tool into a contextually aware suggestion and navigation engine. It is powered by a custom in-house model trained from scratch to optimize for speed and flow awareness. Windsurf Tab is offered in two modes:

| Mode | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Autocomplete** | Traditional line-by-line code completion that predicts the next tokens based on the current context |
| **Supercomplete** | An advanced mode that predicts the developer's intent rather than just the next word. It analyzes code context before and after the cursor and shows suggested changes (both deletions and additions) in a diff box next to the code. Developers press Tab to accept suggestions. |

Additional Tab features include:

- **Tab to Jump:** Introduced in Wave 3, this feature allows developers to navigate to the next logical editing location by pressing Tab, reducing the need for manual cursor movement.
- **Tab to Import:** Added in Wave 4, this automatically suggests and imports necessary modules or libraries with a single Tab press.

### Indexing Engine

The Indexing Engine powers Windsurf's codebase awareness capabilities. Unlike traditional context-aware tools that only consider recently opened files, the Indexing Engine retrieves context from the entire codebase. It uses abstract syntax trees to understand code structure while limiting unnecessary exposure of code content, which also serves as a privacy and security safeguard.

The Indexing Engine supports both local indexing (running entirely on the developer's machine) and remote indexing (for larger codebases that benefit from server-side processing). This architecture significantly improves the quality of both autocomplete suggestions and Cascade's responses.

### Memories

Windsurf's Memories feature enables persistent context across coding sessions. Memories allow Windsurf to remember coding style preferences, team conventions, project-specific rules, and important context from previous sessions. This ensures continuity when developers return to a project, eliminating the need to re-explain context that was already established in earlier conversations.

Developers can configure custom rules for Cascade to follow, such as specifying preferred frameworks, coding standards, response languages, or API conventions. These rules are stored as Memories and applied across all future interactions.

### Custom Rules

Users can define their own custom rules for Cascade to follow during development. Rules can specify:

- Preferred programming languages or frameworks
- Coding style guidelines and naming conventions
- API usage patterns
- Response language preferences
- Project-specific architectural constraints

### Browser Integration

Windsurf includes browser integration capabilities that allow Cascade to interact with web content. The AI assistant can chat about open browser tabs, take screenshots of running web applications, and incorporate console logs and error messages directly into its context. This is particularly useful for frontend development workflows where visual feedback is essential.

## AI Models

### Proprietary Models

Windsurf has developed its own family of AI models specifically designed for software engineering tasks.

#### SWE-1 Family (May 2025)

Released on May 15, 2025, as part of Wave 9, the SWE-1 family introduced the concept of "flow awareness," a framework that enables models to reason over long-running, multi-surface engineering tasks with incomplete or evolving states. The models were trained on user interactions from Windsurf's own editor and incorporate contextual awareness from terminals, browsers, and user feedback loops.

| Model | Target Use Case | Availability |
|-------|----------------|--------------|
| **SWE-1** | Advanced reasoning and tool use for complex engineering tasks | Paid users only |
| **SWE-1-lite** | General-purpose coding assistance (replaced the earlier Cascade Base model) | All users |
| **SWE-1-mini** | Compact, high-speed model for passive prediction in Windsurf Tab | All users |

Based on internal evaluations, SWE-1's performance approached that of frontier models from major foundation model labs, including [Claude](/wiki/claude) 3.5 Sonnet, [GPT-4](/wiki/gpt-4)o, and Gemini 2.5 Pro.

#### SWE-1.5 (October 2025)

Released on October 29, 2025, SWE-1.5 represents a significant leap in both performance and speed. The model is a frontier-size model with hundreds of billions of parameters, built using a mixture-of-experts (MoE) architecture that activates only the relevant specialized sub-networks for each task.

**Key specifications:**

- **Speed:** 950 tokens per second when served on Cerebras hardware, making it 13 times faster than Claude Sonnet 4.5 and 6 times faster than Claude Haiku 4.5
- **Benchmark performance:** Achieved a score of 40.08% on the SWE-Bench Pro benchmark from Scale AI, approaching Claude Sonnet 4.5's leading score of 43.60% but at dramatically higher throughput
- **Training:** Developed using end-to-end [reinforcement learning](/wiki/reinforcement_learning) on real task environments with a custom Cascade agent harness on top of a leading open-source base model
- **Architecture:** Co-designed with its inference system and agent harness as a unified system, optimizing the entire stack from model weights to serving infrastructure

| Model | SWE-Bench Pro Score | Speed (tokens/sec) |
|-------|-------------------|--------------------|
| SWE-1.5 | 40.08% | 950 |
| Claude Sonnet 4.5 | 43.60% | 69 |
| Claude Haiku 4.5 | 39.45% | 142 |
| GPT-5 High | 36.30% | 43 |

In Wave 13 (December 2025), SWE-1.5 Free was made available to all users at no cost for three months, offering the same intelligence and coding performance as the full SWE-1.5 model but at standard throughput speeds.

### Third-Party Models

In addition to its proprietary models, Windsurf provides access to models from other providers:

- [OpenAI](/wiki/openai) models, including GPT-4o and GPT-5
- [Anthropic](/wiki/anthropic) models, including Claude 3.5 Sonnet and Claude Sonnet 4.5
- [Google](/wiki/google) models, including Gemini
- Base models built on Meta's [Llama](/wiki/llama_(language_model)) 3.1 (70B and 405B variants)

The Llama 3.1 70B-based model is available for unlimited use to all users, while the 405B-based premier model offers the highest performance through integration with Windsurf's reasoning engine and native workflows.

### Arena Mode

In early 2026, Windsurf introduced Arena Mode, a feature that allows developers to compare AI models side by side during development. This enables users to evaluate which model produces the best results for their specific use case without committing to a single option.

## Security and Privacy

Windsurf places significant emphasis on enterprise-grade security and data privacy.

### Zero Data Retention

On Enterprise and Teams plans, zero data retention (ZDR) is the default setting. Code data is never stored in logs or used for training unless features like remote indexing or Memories are explicitly enabled by the user. Users on all plans can opt out of code snippet telemetry at any time. All communication between the client and server is encrypted.

### Compliance and Certifications

|--------------------------|--------|
| SOC 2 Type II | Accredited |
| FedRAMP High | Accredited |
| HIPAA | BAA available |
| SAML SSO | Available on Teams and Enterprise |

### Deployment Options

Windsurf supports three deployment models to accommodate different security requirements:

| Deployment Mode | Description | Best For |
|----------------|-------------|----------|

| **Hybrid** | Keeps sensitive data within customer infrastructure while accessing Windsurf features | Regulated industries |
| **Self-Hosted** | Runs inside a customer-managed, GPU-enabled tenant; connects to a trusted LLM endpoint | Maximum security and compliance |

## Technical Architecture

Windsurf is a standalone desktop application built on top of the VS Code open-source codebase (similar to how [Cursor](/wiki/cursor_rules) is also based on VS Code). This architectural choice provides several advantages:

- **Extension compatibility:** Windsurf supports the vast majority of VS Code extensions, allowing developers to bring their existing workflows and tools.
- **Familiar interface:** Developers already accustomed to VS Code can transition to Windsurf with minimal friction.
- **Deep AI integration:** By forking the editor rather than building a simple extension, Windsurf can integrate AI capabilities at a deeper level than a plugin architecture would allow.

### Supported Languages

Windsurf supports over 70 programming languages, including but not limited to:

| Category | Languages |
|----------|-----------|
| Web | JavaScript, TypeScript, HTML, CSS |
| Systems | C, C++, Rust, Go |
| Data Science | [Python](/wiki/python_(programming_language)), R, Julia |
| Enterprise | Java, C#, Kotlin, Scala |
| Mobile | Swift, Dart |
| Scripting | Ruby, PHP, Perl, Lua, Shell |
| Other | Haskell, Elixir, Clojure, OCaml |

The product works best with popular languages like Python, JavaScript, TypeScript, Java, Rust, C++, and Go, where the training data and model specialization are strongest.

### VS Code Extension

In addition to the standalone Windsurf Editor, the company maintains a VS Code extension (formerly known as the Codeium extension) available through the Visual Studio Marketplace. This extension provides Windsurf's AI capabilities to developers who prefer to remain in their existing VS Code environment rather than switching to the standalone editor.

### Market Positioning

Windsurf differentiates itself primarily through its Cascade agent and flow-oriented design philosophy. While Cursor emphasizes giving developers granular control over AI-generated suggestions, Windsurf leans more heavily into autonomous workflows where Cascade can independently reason about architecture, make multi-file changes, and execute commands. [GitHub Copilot](/wiki/github_copilot) remains the market leader by installed base, having crossed 1.8 million paid users by early 2026, but is generally perceived as more focused on fast autocomplete than agentic capabilities. [Claude Code](/wiki/claude_code) takes a different approach entirely, operating as a terminal-based tool that handles tasks more like delegating work to a colleague.

Cursor, backed by a $9.9 billion Series B valuation and over 500,000 paid subscribers, is often considered Windsurf's most direct competitor. Both are standalone IDEs built on VS Code, both offer agentic AI features, and both target professional developers who want AI deeply integrated into their workflow. Windsurf's advantages include lower pricing ($15/month versus $20/month for Cursor Pro) and its proprietary SWE-1 model family, while Cursor is frequently praised for offering more model flexibility and fine-grained developer control.

## Integration with Cognition and Devin

Following Cognition's acquisition, the combined company began integrating Windsurf's IDE capabilities with Devin, Cognition's autonomous AI software engineer. The integration vision includes enabling developers to:

- Plan tasks in an IDE powered by Devin's codebase understanding
- Delegate chunks of work to multiple Devin instances running in parallel
- Complete the highest-leverage parts of development themselves, assisted by Windsurf's autocomplete and Cascade
- Review AI-generated pull requests and code changes within a single unified interface

Wave 12 introduced the initial Devin integration, including a new UI and access to Devin's capabilities from within the Windsurf Editor. By early 2026, the combined Cognition/Windsurf platform had rolled out support for GPT-5.2 with limited-time zero-credit access and made it the default model across both Windsurf and core Devin workloads.

Since the acquisition, Cognition's combined enterprise ARR has grown by more than 30%, and the company was valued at $10.2 billion in September 2025.
