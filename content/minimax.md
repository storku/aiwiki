MiniMax** ([Chinese](/index.php?title=Chinese_language&action=edit&redlink=1): 上海稀宇科技有限公司, Shanghai Xiyu Technology Co., Ltd.) is a [private](/index.php?title=Private_company&action=edit&redlink=1) [artificial intelligence](/wiki/artificial_intelligence) (AI) company headquartered in [Shanghai](/index.php?title=Shanghai&action=edit&redlink=1), [China](/index.php?title=China&action=edit&redlink=1). Founded in December 2021 by former [SenseTime](/index.php?title=SenseTime&action=edit&redlink=1) executives, the company has been dubbed one of China's "AI Tiger" companies by investors as of 2024.[&#91;3&#93;](#cite_note-Bloomberg2025-3) MiniMax develops [large language models](/wiki/large_language_model), multimodal AI systems, and consumer-facing AI applications. As of 2025, MiniMax's products have served over 157 million individual users across over 200 countries and regions, and more than 50,000 enterprises and developers across over 90 countries and regions.[&#91;2&#93;](#cite_note-Reuters2025IPO-2)

## History

### Founding and Early Development

MiniMax was founded in December 2021 by several computer vision veterans from [SenseTime](/index.php?title=SenseTime&action=edit&redlink=1):[&#91;4&#93;](#cite_note-BambooWorks2023-4)[&#91;5&#93;](#cite_note-AsiaTechLens2025-5)

- **Yan Junjie (闫俊杰)** - Former Vice President at SenseTime, responsible for building deep learning toolchains and underlying algorithms, now serves as CEO

- **Yang Bin (杨斌)** - Previously co-founded the research center for [Uber](/index.php?title=Uber&action=edit&redlink=1) Advanced Technologies Group and worked at [Waabi](/index.php?title=Waabi&action=edit&redlink=1)

- **Zhou Yucong (周雨丛)** - Former head of algorithms R&D at SenseTime

When it first started out, MiniMax received funding from [MiHoYo](/index.php?title=MiHoYo&action=edit&redlink=1), the Chinese video game developer known for [Genshin Impact](/index.php?title=Genshin_Impact&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;6&#93;](#cite_note-Reuters2023-6)

On July 16, 2025, [Reuters](/index.php?title=Reuters&action=edit&redlink=1) reported that MiniMax had confidentially filed for an [initial public offering](/index.php?title=Initial_public_offering&action=edit&redlink=1) (IPO) on the [Hong Kong Stock Exchange](/index.php?title=Hong_Kong_Stock_Exchange&action=edit&redlink=1), aiming to raise between HK$4 billion and HK$5 billion (US$510-637 million) and targeting a valuation above US$4 billion. ## Products and Services

### Consumer Applications

#### Glow and Its Successors

MiniMax's first product was **[Glow](/index.php?title=Glow&action=edit&redlink=1)**, launched in October 2022, which allowed users to create virtual characters, give them background stories and chat with them about various topics. Within four months of launch, the app had over 5 million users. Due to filing issues, Glow was terminated in March 2023.[&#91;11&#93;](#cite_note-SCMP2023-11)

Glow was relaunched under two new brands:

- **[Talkie](/index.php?title=Talkie&action=edit&redlink=1)** for international markets (June 2023)

- **[Xing Ye](/index.php?title=Xing_Ye&action=edit&redlink=1) (星野)** for the Chinese market (September 2023)

For June 2024, Talkie ranked fifth among the most-downloaded free entertainment apps in the U.S., with more than half of its 11 million monthly active users in the U.S. Other popular markets included the Philippines, the U.K., and Canada.[&#91;12&#93;](#cite_note-WSJ2024-12) The app features AI avatars of public figures, including [Donald Trump](/index.php?title=Donald_Trump&action=edit&redlink=1), [Taylor Swift](/index.php?title=Taylor_Swift&action=edit&redlink=1), [Elon Musk](/index.php?title=Elon_Musk&action=edit&redlink=1), and [LeBron James](/index.php?title=LeBron_James&action=edit&redlink=1).[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-SCMPTalkie2024-13)

Talkie was pulled from Apple's App Store in December 2024 for unspecified "technical" reasons but later returned.[&#91;13&#93;](#cite_note-SCMPTalkie2024-13)

#### Hailuo AI (海螺AI)

Launched in March 2024, **[Hailuo AI](/index.php?title=Hailuo_AI&action=edit&redlink=1)** is a multimodal large language model consumer platform that provides AI text, video, and music-generating features. Key releases include:

- **Video-01**: A text-to-video model launched in September 2024

- **T2V-01-Director** and **I2V-01-Director**: Models released on January 28, 2025, designed to revolutionize video creation with professional filmmaking tools

- Audio functions launched on January 20, 2025[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-MinimaxOfficial-14)

#### Hai Luo (海螺) Productivity Tool

**[Hai Luo](/index.php?title=Hai_Luo&action=edit&redlink=1)** is an AI-powered productivity and copilot tool, similar to [Microsoft Copilot](/wiki/microsoft_copilot). It can perform tasks like document summarization, content creation, and code generation. It supports processing long documents up to 200,000 words.[&#91;15&#93;](#cite_note-36kr2024-15)

### Technical Achievements

#### MiniMax-M1

MiniMax-M1, released in June 2025, represents a breakthrough in computational efficiency. The entire reinforcement learning phase used only 512 H800s for three weeks, with a rental cost of just $534,700. The model requires just 30% of the computing power needed by rival [DeepSeek](/wiki/deepseek)'s R1 model when performing deep reasoning tasks.[&#91;18&#93;](#cite_note-Computerworld2025-18)

### API and Enterprise Services

MiniMax provides enterprise API services through its Open API Platform, serving over 50,000 enterprises and developers globally. The company offers competitive pricing, with input processing costing just $0.4 per million tokens for contexts up to 200,000 tokens, rising to $1.3 per million tokens for the full 1-million-token capability.[&#91;14&#93;](#cite_note-MinimaxOfficial-14)

## Corporate Structure

MiniMax operates through its parent company, Shanghai Xiyu Jizhi Technology Co., Ltd. (上海稀宇极智科技有限公司), which was established in 2021. The company is a subsidiary of MiniMax HONGKONG Limited with 100% ownership.[&#91;19&#93;](#cite_note-PitchBook-19)

### Industry Outlook

In 2024, MiniMax founder Yan Junjie predicted that "in the future, there will only be five large model companies left in the world," noting that the ratio of the AI market between internet companies and AI startups may reach 9:1, more extreme than the 6:4 ratio seen in the transition from internet to mobile internet.[&#91;20&#93;](#cite_note-iNews2024-20)

## Controversies

### Copyright Infringement Lawsuit

In September 2025, [Disney](/index.php?title=Disney&action=edit&redlink=1), [Warner Bros. Discovery](/index.php?title=Warner_Bros._Discovery&action=edit&redlink=1), and [NBCUniversal](/index.php?title=NBCUniversal&action=edit&redlink=1) filed a joint copyright infringement lawsuit against MiniMax and its parent company Shanghai Xiyu Jizhi Technology Co. Ltd., alleging "willful and brazen" copyright infringement through its Hailuo AI service. The studios alleged that MiniMax markets Hailuo AI as a "Hollywood studio in your pocket" and built its business from intellectual property stolen from Hollywood studios.[&#91;21&#93;](#cite_note-Variety2025-21)[&#91;22&#93;](#cite_note-Bloomberg2025Lawsuit-22)

In December 2024, Broadcast magazine reported that Hailuo AI can reproduce the logos of British television channels [Channel 4](/index.php?title=Channel_4&action=edit&redlink=1), [Channel 5](/index.php?title=Channel_5&action=edit&redlink=1), and [ITV](/index.php?title=ITV&action=edit&redlink=1) in its AI-generated videos.[&#91;23&#93;](#cite_note-Broadcast2024-23)

### Other Legal Issues

MiniMax is reportedly being sued by [iQiyi](/index.php?title=IQiyi&action=edit&redlink=1), a Chinese video streaming service that alleges MiniMax illicitly trained on iQiyi's copyrighted recordings.[&#91;24&#93;](#cite_note-iQiyiLawsuit-24)

## Technical Infrastructure

### Open Source Strategy

In January 2025, MiniMax released its MiniMax-01 model family, including weights and code. CEO Yan Junjie admitted he would have gone open-source from day one if he could choose again, as the decision invites developer collaboration while showcasing technical capabilities.[&#91;25&#93;](#cite_note-SCMP2025OpenSource-25)
